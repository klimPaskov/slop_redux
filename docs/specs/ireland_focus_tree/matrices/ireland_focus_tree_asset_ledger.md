# Ireland Focus Tree Asset Ledger

Feature slug: `ireland_focus_tree`

Status: Part 6 authoritative asset planning ledger

# Source clearance and archive hierarchy

Catalogue discovery does not count as permission to use an image. Every sourced item receives one clearance state and one source-tier record before it can enter a final asset manifest.

| State | Clearance meaning | Permitted planning use | Final asset rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| C0 discovered only | a candidate or catalogue record exists, but provenance and rights are not established | research lead only | cannot be downloaded, processed, published, or treated as available |
| C1 provenance identified | creator, archive, date, and object identity are substantially known, but rights remain unclear | comparison, crop planning, and source evaluation | cannot enter a public or mod package until rights are resolved |
| C2 restricted or review pending | the source has terms, fees, access restrictions, uncertain jurisdiction, or required approval | internal review and user decision | no final DDS until the restriction is satisfied and recorded |
| C3 cleared with conditions | permission, licence, or institutional terms allow use with attribution, limits, or other conditions | source and processed previews under the recorded conditions | final use requires exact credit, licence text, and any distribution limits in the manifest |
| C4 cleared for unrestricted project use | public-domain status or explicit permission is verified for the intended use and distribution | full production | final source, processed PNG, DDS, credit, provenance, and verification record are required |

Archive and source hierarchy guides confidence and search order. A lower tier can still be valid when its provenance and rights are stronger than a higher-tier candidate.

| Rank | Source tier | Typical examples | Validation rule |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Irish official archives and government repositories | Military Archives, National Archives of Ireland, Oireachtas, Irish Statute Book, President, Met Éireann, CSO | prefer original catalogue and rights statement |
| 2 | Northern Irish and British official archives | PRONI, UK National Archives, Imperial War Museums and official service collections | verify jurisdiction, catalogue identity, and reuse terms |
| 3 | national libraries and major public collections | National Library of Ireland, British Library, Library of Congress and Europeana source institutions | follow the holding institution rather than aggregator shorthand |
| 4 | universities and recognised research archives | university special collections, folklore, labour, party, diocesan, industrial, and regional archives | record collection, item, date, repository, and access conditions |
| 5 | museums and established heritage institutions | national, military, transport, maritime, industrial, local and community museums | distinguish object photography rights from object copyright |
| 6 | political, labour, religious, cultural, and institutional repositories | party archives, unions, Church bodies, Conradh na Gaeilge, movement collections | verify that the repository can license the specific item |
| 7 | reputable digitised public-domain repositories | Wikimedia Commons and equivalent services with full primary-source metadata | trace to the original holding source and confirm the stated licence |
| 8 | commercial archives and press agencies | licensed news, photographic, film, map, and document agencies | require explicit project-compatible licence and cost approval |
| 9 | published academic or documentary works | books, journals, catalogues and documentaries used to identify primary sources | source lead only unless the included item has separate permission |
| 10 | private collections and user-provided material | family archives, collectors and user uploads | require ownership declaration, provenance, permission, and privacy review |
| 11 | open-web discovery sources | search results, blogs, forums, social media and unsourced mirrors | lead only. Never infer rights, identity, date, or authenticity from the mirror |

Each per-item manifest records the holding source, catalogue or object identifier, creator, date or date range, source URL, rights holder, licence or permission, clearance state, required credit, intended crop and use, uncertainty, rejected alternatives, source path, processed path, final DDS path, and intended sprite name.

# Asset production acceptance tests

1. No real person receives a generated likeness.
2. The 40 historical symbol and document packages record reuse checks, provenance, date and rights confidence.
3. Every historical symbol records source, date, archive and licence confidence.
4. Every alternate asset is labelled generated and begins after the divergence point.
5. Every implemented focus has a valid icon and no branch uses one generic icon repeatedly.
6. Achievement filenames match full achievement IDs and include all three states.
7. Flag files follow engine root and size conventions rather than feature folders.
8. All other feature-owned assets use `ireland_focus_tree` folders where engine conventions allow.
9. All seven animations have real source frames and static fallbacks.
10. The 120-image allocation supports the event catalogue through documented family reuse.
11. Placeholder art is not accepted as completion.
