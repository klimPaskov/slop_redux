Historical classification used throughout:

- `H` means documented history or a direct representation of a documented institution, actor, election, law, dispute, or political position.

This matrix cross-checks the prose specification, National Settlement map, and constitutional route map. It is an implementation acceptance ledger. It must not be used to shrink the mapped routes into a small collection of modifiers.

The 1936 ledger is a controlled reconstruction from the 1933 election and the later Fine Gael merger. Formal seats determine chamber totals. Routine confidence votes determine government arithmetic. Candidate movement, by-elections, deaths, defections, and local organisation may change both ledgers through events before the 1937 election.

The seat model uses integers and must always sum to the active chamber size. Party popularity alone cannot calculate the result. The model also reads local organisation, transfer affinity, candidate quality, campaign promises, route performance, economic pressure, crisis handling, and government credibility.
