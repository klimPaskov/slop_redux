## Legend

- `[H]` documented historical foundation
- `[P]` plausible alternate-history development
- `[A]` deliberate absurdity
- `=>` strong progression
- `->` optional progression or support connection
- `X` major incompatibility
- `~` route changes the meaning or rewards of a shared lane
- `?` hidden reveal

## Opening architecture

The opening centre is designed as a broad fan rather than a single vertical chain.

```text
                         [H] Government of the Young State
                                      |
            +-------------------------+--------------------------+
            |                         |                          |
 [H] Civil War Settlement   [H] Economic War Review    [H] Defence and Sovereignty Review
            |                         |                          |
     +------+-------+          +------+-------+            +-----+------+
     |              |          |              |            |            |
reconcile        enforce     protect       negotiate    army audit   port and coast audit
veterans         state       domestic      trade        and command  and communications
     |              |          |              |            |            |
     +------+-------+          +------+-------+            +-----+------+
            |                         |                          |
            +-------------- [H/P] Constitutional Convention ----+
                                      |
                      +---------------+---------------+
                      |                               |
            [H/P] constitutional adoption      [P] constitutional crisis
                      |                               |
                      +---------------+---------------+
                                      |
                      [H/P] Trade and Treaty Port Settlement
```

Opening design rules:

1. The player can complete only part of every side branch before the main constitutional gateway.
2. Early decisions alter later focus cost, mission difficulty, and stakeholder strength.
3. No visible major route should be locked by one opening focus alone.
4. A constitutional crisis opens emergency content without deleting economic or defence progress.
5. Support branches should remain useful after a government change.

### Political family boundaries

| Family | Compatible support | Conditional bridge | Hard conflict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Fianna Fáil constitutional | most economy, defence, culture, neutrality | Labour coalition, republican reconciliation, Allied shift | full O'Duffy or Ailtirí takeover without government collapse |
| Fine Gael constitutional | trade, professional army, cultural policy, Allied or neutral diplomacy | Labour coalition, limited vocational settlement | IRA seizure and total corporate takeover |
| Labour parliamentary | social economy, citizen defence, active neutrality, federal unity | Republican Congress if radical networks grow | O'Duffy, Ailtirí, clerical domination |
| Republican Congress | social economy, people's army, labour internationalism | Labour parliamentary transition, civilianisation after revolution | O'Duffy, Ailtirí, hard clerical order |
| IRA Army Council | guerrilla defence, anti-partition expansion, foreign opportunism | reconciliation with state or Congress split | British-aligned constitutional order |
| Vocational constitutional | agrarian and corporate economy, church diplomacy, territorial defence | Fine Gael or Fianna Fáil constitutional compromise | workers' revolution and secular coercion |
| O'Duffy corporate | corporate economy, veterans, militarisation, anti-communist diplomacy | Ailtirí infiltration or church compact | parliamentary Labour and Republican Congress |
| Ailtirí | coercive culture, total mobilisation, pan-Celtic extremism | hidden mythic corruption or internal coup | pluralist parliamentary settlement |
| Hidden serious culture lane | almost every route in moderate form | mythic reveal when conditions combine | none until a hidden transformation is chosen |

## Architecture handoff to Part 2

Part 2 must begin with the full design of `The National Settlement`. It should define:

- exact stages and route-adaptive labels
- stakeholder contributions
- change sources
- threshold effects
- crisis triggers
- leader, party, law, focus, decision, mission, and event links
- AI behavior
- presentation choice
- cleanup and transformation rules

After the mechanic is complete, Part 2 should map the constitutional political families in this order:

1. Fianna Fáil and de Valera
2. Lemass developmental succession
3. Fine Gael and constitutional opposition
4. Dillon and Allied intervention
5. Labour parliamentary government
6. church and vocational influence across those routes

Part 2 must not yet draft the full IRA, O'Duffy, Ailtirí, or mythic branches beyond the interfaces needed to preserve their future access.
