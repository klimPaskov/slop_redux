# Ireland Focus Tree Constitutional Routes Map

Feature slug: `ireland_focus_tree`

Planning status: Part 2 of 7

This file maps the constitutional political crown that grows from the shared 1936 opening. It is a route and branch architecture document. It does not set final focus coordinates, final focus count, implementation identifiers, or final localisation.

All route, focus, decision, mission, event, idea, law, and conclusion names are working labels.

Classification markers:

- `H` means documented history
- `P` means plausible speculation grounded in period actors and institutions
- `A` means deliberate absurdity

No `A` route is drafted here. Part 3 receives explicit hidden-route interfaces.

## Reading the architecture

The tree uses one political crown with shared gateways and route-adaptive side branches.

Political routes do not exist as isolated vertical ladders.

Every route interacts with:

- the National Settlement
- elections and government formation
- cabinet and party lifecycles
- Church, labour, rural, minority, and security stakeholders
- the 1937 constitutional campaign
- the 1938 sovereignty settlement
- economy, defence, neutrality, diplomacy, and Northern Ireland support lanes
- events, missions, and route conclusions

## Inherited mechanic contract

This route map does not redefine the National Settlement. It carries the following accepted opening contract from the mechanic map so that route locks and handoffs cannot silently change the starting state.

| Surface | Opening state | Route-map use |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| National Settlement | `+25` | every route begins from the same constitutional-state advantage and preserves the recorded path before pole conversion |
| Settlement Momentum | `-1` | the opening state drifts toward unresolved revolutionary pressure until the player creates a valid settlement response |
| Social Consent | `55` | major constitutional projects require bargaining and delivery rather than executive authority alone |
| National Readiness | `15` | sovereignty and war-policy routes inherit severe defence weakness and depend on Part 4 capacity building |
| British Leverage | `45` | trade, ports, defence, Northern Ireland, and wartime access shape route feasibility |
| Vatican Leverage | `25` | foreign religious pressure remains distinct from domestic Church Institutional Influence |
| American Leverage | `5` | Atlantic economic and diplomatic support begins limited and grows through later systems |
| German Leverage | `5` | German pressure and opportunities begin limited and become relevant through war, trade, intelligence, or radical routes |

The presentation remains a native balance-of-power bar paired with an evolving decision category and scripted-localisation breakdowns. A normal-play custom scripted GUI is not required for the Part 2 constitutional routes.

## Shared opening crown

### Opening lane A: `A State Still Being Defined`

Purpose:

- introduces the National Settlement
- establishes party seats and stakeholders
- starts the 1937 constitutional timetable
- reveals Cabinet Cohesion, Dáil Mandate, and Social Consent

Representative focus groups:

- survey the institutions of the state
- prepare constitutional consultation
- address Civil War administrative memory
- define the relationship between government and republican legitimacy
- establish the election and referendum timetable

Primary rewards:

- decision families
- stakeholder consultations
- election missions
- law review
- constitutional events

Avoid:

- direct party-seat rewards
- one-focus ideology changes
- instant removal of Civil War pressure

### Opening parliamentary ledger

| Bloc | Formal seats | Routine confidence votes | Route consequence |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Fianna Fáil | `77` | `76` | governs from the documented party total while the outgoing Ceann Comhairle remains outside routine party voting |
| Fine Gael inheritance | `59` | `59` | begins as a combined constitutional opposition inheritance from Cumann na nGaedheal and the National Centre Party |
| Labour | `8` | `8` | holds independent confidence leverage without becoming an automatic government appendage |
| independents and minor deputies | `9` | `9` | local and farmer interests create issue-based confidence and coalition possibilities |
| total | `153` | `152` | the one-vote difference is the outgoing Ceann Comhairle reserve and not a missing seat |

The formal chamber contains `153` seats and uses `77` as the opening majority benchmark. The `Ceann Comhairle Reserve` is handled through parliamentary procedure and cannot be used as a normal focus reward, coalition promise, or hidden government vote.

### Opening lane B: `The Constitution Before the Country`

Purpose:

- manages drafting and plebiscite politics
- links President, Seanad, religion, minority rights, territory, language, and emergency authority
- determines the first mandate of the new constitutional order

Internal forks:

- strong executive or stronger review
- explicit Catholic recognition or broader civic formula
- territorial claim language or cautious partition wording
- language aspiration with capacity or symbolic overreach
- emergency competence with review or broad executive discretion

Convergence:

- all constitutional routes receive a plebiscite result and constitutional law state
- rejection opens replacement drafting rather than deleting the tree

### Opening lane C: `The Sovereignty Settlement`

Purpose:

- links the Economic War, trade, Treaty Ports, defence responsibility, and British leverage
- creates the 1938 gateway

Internal forks:

- rapid settlement or continued economic pressure
- unrestricted sovereignty language or negotiated technical cooperation
- immediate defence investment or political use of the transfer

Convergence:

- the ports return only through the negotiated settlement or a later alternate route
- the transfer creates National Readiness obligations

### Opening lane D: `Government and Opposition`

Purpose:

- prepares Fianna Fáil, Fine Gael, Labour, independents, and minor groups for the 1937 election
- separates party organisation from ideology popularity
- introduces coalition and confidence mechanics

Route preparation:

- Fianna Fáil can defend government, reconcile republican networks, or build a development faction
- Fine Gael can close the O'Duffy chapter and rebuild constitutional credibility
- Labour can organise a social programme and preserve movement unity
- Dillon can begin an internal dissent ledger without opening the full Allied route

## Fianna Fáil route family `[H with bounded P branches]`

### Fianna Fáil shared identity

Fianna Fáil enters government with a dual inheritance.

- it controls the constitutional state
- it still claims continuity with the republican revolution

Its shared route asks whether government authority can complete independence without allowing party, executive, or emergency power to substitute for the state.

### Shared route opener: `The Republican Government`

Role:

- confirms Fianna Fáil as a constitutional governing party
- transforms revolutionary inheritance into a managed legitimacy resource
- opens party, cabinet, republican, and constitutional sublanes

Required state:

- Fianna Fáil forms or retains government
- no revolutionary takeover has occurred
- Dáil Mandate remains valid

Reward style:

- route-specific National Settlement labels
- cabinet and party decisions
- constitutional legitimacy missions
- advisor access

### Shared lane: `The Constitution Becomes the State`

Focus groups:

- implement the constitutional settlement
- establish presidency and Seanad working relationships
- publish administrative guidance
- define legal continuity and institutional succession
- submit emergency competence to a clear constitutional basis

Branch interaction:

- stronger legal review supports later stewardship and reconciliation
- stronger executive drafting supports emergency direction while increasing overreach risk
- minority and religious guarantees alter Church and Northern reactions

### Shared lane: `A Party of Government`

Focus groups:

- county organisation and candidate discipline

- succession preparation

Failure:

### Shared lane: `Republican Legitimacy`

Focus groups:

- commemorate independence under constitutional authority

- selective reintegration

Mechanic:

- Republican Network Strength

### Shared lane: `Neutrality by Irish Decision`

Narrative role:

Focus groups:

Distinct gameplay:

### De Valera presidency variant `[P]`

A late voluntary leadership transition can allow de Valera to seek the presidency.

Conditions:

- a valid successor leads government
- no active constitutional crisis
- Dáil and party transition completed
- presidency election or nomination process occurs lawfully

Effects:

- de Valera leaves ordinary government control
- the presidency gains a stronger political shadow without becoming an executive office
- successor governments face consultation and legitimacy events

The variant cannot be used to keep de Valera as both governing Taoiseach and constitutional president.

## Lemass developmental succession `[P rooted in H]`

### Lemass route promise

The Lemass route replaces inherited revolutionary authority with administrative delivery, industrial planning, supply competence, and a negotiated developmental coalition.

It cannot begin through a one-focus deposition.

### Developmental Standing

Range: `0` to `6`

Standing sources:

- successful industry and commerce programme
- delivery during shortage or emergency
- cabinet support
- county organisation reform
- Labour or rural compact
- election performance
- de Valera's voluntary transition or political retirement

Standing losses:

- supply failure
- county backlash
- union rupture
- foreign dependency
- public leadership challenge without party support

### Succession windows

| Window | Classification | Conditions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| early post-1938 | `P` | Standing `4+`, sovereignty settlement complete, valid de Valera transition, cabinet and party compact |
| wartime administrative | `P` | severe supply pressure, Standing `4+`, cabinet need, lawful party and Dáil process |
| normal late transition | `H` trajectory beyond core period | de Valera retirement and party succession process |

### Three-stage succession

1. cabinet compact
2. party conference and county organisation
3. Dáil nomination and government formation

Failure at any stage creates a truce, delayed succession, party fracture, or route defeat. It does not award leadership.

### Route transformation

Positive pole: `Developmental State Capacity`

Negative pole: `Traditional and Revolutionary Constraint`

Sustainable target: `+20` to `+60`

### Foundation choices

The route needs support from at least two foundations before accelerated development.

| Foundation | Contribution | Cost or veto |
| --- | --- | --- |

| Labour and unions | wage peace and workforce legitimacy | bargaining rights and social spending |
| rural and cooperative organisations | county legitimacy and food policy | regional investment and price concessions |
| Church and service networks | education and welfare reach | inspection, moral, and service-dependency disputes |
| Atlantic trade and foreign partners | capital, machinery, and markets | External Leverage and sovereignty safeguards |

## James Dillon constitutional Allied route `[H dissent with P government outcome]`

### Dillon route identity

Dillon argues that neutrality can become morally and strategically untenable. His route requires public dissent, parliamentary persuasion, defence preparation, and explicit sovereignty safeguards.

It is not the normal Fine Gael route.

Positive pole: `Parliamentary War Mandate`

Negative pole: `Neutrality Consensus`

### Reveal sequence

1. foreign war and neutrality debate
2. Dillon public dissent
3. party conflict
4. possible 1942 rupture or earlier crisis divergence
5. intervention caucus
6. public and parliamentary campaign
7. war or cooperation vote

### Intervention Mandate

A route value tracks support from:

- Dáil deputies
- cabinet or coalition partners
- public opinion
- defence establishment
- Church and moral authorities
- Britain and the United States

Support from foreign actors also raises leverage unless safeguards exist.

### Required conditions for formal belligerency

- Intervention Mandate at least `90`
- National Readiness at least `45`
- Social Consent at least `60`
- a named Dáil war-assent vote
- a government retaining majority support
- British Leverage below `60`
- a written sovereignty safeguard package with at least three accepted clauses
- a Northern Ireland policy or formally accepted deferral
- no active lost-majority, dual-authority, or emergency-overreach crisis

### Focus phases

#### Phase 1: public dissent

- moral and strategic case
- document neutrality failures and violations
- build cross-party contacts
- accept party consequences

#### Phase 2: parliamentary coalition

- obtain deputy commitments
- negotiate cabinet entry or national government
- prepare readiness and access safeguards
- define war aims and limits

#### Phase 3: conditional cooperation

- intelligence and weather cooperation
- shipping and rescue
- procurement and training
- limited access under Dáil review

#### Phase 4: war assent

- public campaign
- final readiness mission
- Dáil vote
- coalition confidence
- foreign guarantee settlement

### Dillon tradeoffs

- moral urgency versus neutrality consensus
- Allied aid versus leverage
- war preparation versus social and economic burden
- Northern bargaining versus unionist and British limits

### Failure states

- `Independent Voice in an Unmoved Dáil`
- `War Without a Mandate`
- `Protected Dependency`
- `The Allies Will Not Pay the Price`

### Dillon conclusions

| Conclusion | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| constitutional belligerent | Ireland enters war through valid assent and safeguards |
| armed Allied partner short of belligerency | deep cooperation with continued public neutrality and explicit limits |
| independent parliamentary dissent | Dillon fails to realign policy but preserves democratic opposition |

AI:

- rare before severe war conditions
- never chosen as a default Fine Gael focus route
- requires readiness and public mandate
- retreats when leverage exceeds safeguards

### AI focus ordering

Within any route, AI should prefer:

1. active crisis and mission prerequisites
2. valid government and leadership settlement
3. route mechanic stabilisation
4. enabling focus for selected economy, defence, or diplomacy branch
5. cross-route Church or cultural focus that solves an active problem
6. route payoff
7. optional flavour or side branch

AI should not rush a capstone while government formation, emergency cleanup, or coalition promises remain unresolved.
