## Map purpose

This map defines the serious cultural support lane and the three concealed restoration families that can grow from it.

1. High Kingship and Tara restoration
2. Five Provinces and Mide restoration
3. Otherworld compact, sovereignty, guardianship, containment, and dominion

The cultural lane is complete gameplay even when no hidden route is revealed. Concealed content depends on difficult public preparation, route-specific evidence, unusual campaign conditions, explicit disqualifiers, and a clear transition from documented history to plausible speculation to deliberate absurdity.

## Classification boundary

| Class | Meaning in this map | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `H` | documented institution, site, organisation, cultural practice, text tradition, or historical political form | Conradh na Gaeilge, Irish Folklore Commission, Schools' Collection, Tara, Uisneach, Mide, provincial identities, early Irish law study, recorded Otherworld belief |
| `P` | a modern state experiment built from documented institutions and a credible crisis | elected guardian crown, five-region federal convention, office for unexplained reports, protected customary mediation |

## Concealed restoration doctrine

### Reveal stack

A concealed route requires all five layers.

| Layer | Requirement | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. serious capacity | route-specific Cultural Capacity thresholds | prevents absurd content from replacing real cultural work |
| 2. public preparation | surveys, councils, archives, sites, law, and regional institutions | creates a credible state and social framework |
| 3. political opening | constitutional crisis, convention, regional settlement, dispersed government, or other route-specific state | prevents a free peacetime transformation |
| 4. evidence ledger | independent route evidence with custody and source variation | stops one event or one focus from revealing the route |
| 5. unusual campaign conditions | war, evacuation, site threat, simultaneous regional events, or another named state | makes the route rare and campaign-specific |

### Discovery levels

| Level | Player knowledge | Available action | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0. unexamined | ordinary cultural reports only | serious cultural policy | no hidden route |
| 1. pattern noticed | two or more unusual but explainable records | investigate, archive, dismiss | false lead or foreign deception |
| 2. credible anomaly | independent sources survive first review | mixed inquiry, witness protection, site mission | exposure, panic, coercion |
| 3. route hypothesis | evidence points toward crown, province, or Otherworld pattern | assemble convention or specialist office | political capture and fabrication |
| 4. verified threshold | route-specific evidence standard met | reveal event and explicit choice | route becomes visible, disqualifiers lock |
| 5. public transformation | government and society accept a new order | route focus family and state transition | full obligations, failures, and foreign reaction |

### Shared evidence ledgers

| Ledger | Used by | Examples | Credibility rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| constitutional and legal evidence | High Kingship, Five Provinces | ratified mandates, historical commission, office powers, succession plan | modern political action required |
| genealogical and claimant evidence | High Kingship | pedigree, conduct, finance, foreign ties, regional support | no single family record is decisive |
| provincial evidence | Five Provinces | regional convention, historical association, county participation, middle-region proposal | mandates must come from modern communities |
| archival correspondence | all concealed routes | independent manuscripts, school records, field notes, sealed collections | collector and custody chains must be independent |
| site evidence | High Kingship, Otherworld, Five Provinces | protected royal site, anomaly, access pattern, ritual or public event | site must be protected and reviewed by more than one institution |
| witness evidence | Otherworld | unrelated witnesses, local guardians, officers, doctors, clergy, scientists | no shared interview, publication, or political organiser |
| obligation evidence | High Kingship, Otherworld | recorded warning, accepted geis, linked result | record predates event and has verified custody |

### False lead rules

1. A false lead is a playable investigation with a real ordinary explanation.
2. Foreign intelligence, smuggling, local crime, political staging, weather, memory error, and collector contamination can create false leads.
3. Exposing a false lead improves evidence quality if the state admits the result.
4. Suppressing the correction or rewarding fabrication damages Archival Record and may close the credible route.
5. The player cannot farm evidence from repeated false leads.
6. Every investigation target receives cleanup after resolution.

### Shared disqualifiers

- forged evidence accepted after exposure
- coerced witness testimony used as proof
- unresolved collection-without-consent failure
- major site deliberately destroyed for political or military gain
- route institution fully captured by Ailtirí or another movement without later liberation
- foreign sponsor appoints the hidden government
- hidden route already failed through a permanent disqualifier
- incompatible authoritarian conclusion that denies the route's required regional or public mandate

### Hidden AI prohibition

Historical AI cannot reveal a concealed route. Rare alternate AI needs all public, political, evidence, and campaign conditions. It must also have a route-specific plan and enough capacity to pay the route's continuing obligations.
