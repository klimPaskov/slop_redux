# Ireland focus tree research notes, part 1

These notes summarize research used for the first planning tranche. They are design notes and should be checked again during final implementation.

## Confirmed anchors used in the design

- The Constitution of Ireland was enacted by the people on 1 July 1937 and came into operation on 29 December 1937. This supports making the constitution a central focus milestone.
- Documents on Irish Foreign Policy records the 1938 Treaty Port transfer discussions and related correspondence. This supports making the ports settlement a major sovereignty and defence branch.
- Irish Military Archives records the Look Out Post log books from 83 coastal LOPs. This supports a coastwatching mission family and Emergency preparedness mechanic.
- History Ireland and other scholarly sources connect O'Duffy and the Blueshirts to Fine Gael, Spain, Italy, and anti communist politics. This supports separating democratic opposition from a route locked corporatist path.
- National Archives UK material records Hermann Goertz as associated with Plan Kathleen. The Irish Story describes Plan Kathleen as an IRA proposal for German invasion of Northern Ireland with IRA support. This supports a dangerous IRA foreign contact branch.
- ESB and Shannon Scheme material supports using Ardnacrusha, electrification, and grid development as an Irish specific industry lane.
- Irish Sugar Company and sugar beet history supports rural industrialization through Carlow, Tuam, Mallow, and Thurles style projects.

## Historical claims requiring caution

- Plan Kathleen details vary between sources and should not be treated as a competent military plan. The design should use it as an intelligence risk and foreign contact crisis rather than as a reliable invasion route.
- Blueshirt politics should not erase the existence of constitutional conservative opposition. The democratic opposition branch and the O'Duffy branch must remain different.
- Labour and anti fascist volunteers should not be collapsed into a generic Soviet route. The democratic Labour path should remain viable.
- A hidden cultural restoration route has not been accepted. It needs a later anti bloat evaluation.

## Source mode implications

- Éamon de Valera, W. T. Cosgrave, Seán Lemass, Eoin O'Duffy, William Norton, Frank Ryan, Seán Russell, Douglas Hyde, and major Northern leaders need sourced portraits if used as leaders or advisors.
- The Irish tricolour and any historical party, militia, or movement symbol require source review before final flag or emblem work.
- Focus icons, decision icons, achievement icons, and fictional route symbols can be generated with asset skill rules.
