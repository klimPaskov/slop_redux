## Douglas Hyde and Gaelic League anchor

The National Library of Ireland page on Douglas Hyde, Eoin MacNeill, and the Gaelic League says Hyde chaired the foundation meeting in 1893, that Eoin MacNeill convened it, and that Hyde became first president. It also describes the organization as non political and non sectarian. This supports a civic route and a common platform settlement branch.

Design use:

- Hyde can be a sourced portrait and cultural patron if visible in country package work
- the cultural route should include cross community civic work, not sectarian pressure
- final text must research exact wording and not invent Gaelic slogans
