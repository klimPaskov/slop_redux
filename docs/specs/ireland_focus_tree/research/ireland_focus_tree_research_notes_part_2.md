# Ireland focus tree research notes, part 2

These notes extend the Part 1 research basis for the detailed branch, decision, formable, and mechanic files. They are not final localisation.

## Constitutional trunk

The 1937 constitution remains the main early sovereignty anchor. It supports a focus group that treats the constitution as a state building project, the presidency as an identity change, and legal legitimacy as a mechanic source.

Useful sources:

- Official Irish Statute Book Constitution of Ireland page. It records the constitution as enacted by the people on 1 July 1937 and as coming into operation on 29 December 1937.
- Douglas Hyde official presidency biography. It supports using the presidency as a visible constitutional milestone and source asset need rather than a generic idea.

## Treaty Ports and Anglo Irish settlement

Treaty Ports should be a branch and decision family because they connect sovereignty, neutrality, British pressure, naval access, and Emergency defence. The ports should not be treated only as an opinion modifier or one time port handover effect.

Useful sources:

- Documents on Irish Foreign Policy material on the 1938 Treaty Ports transfer discussions and the British Irish agreement.
- Official or archival material naming Cobh, Berehaven, and Lough Swilly in the transfer context.

Design consequence:

- The port issue should unlock named port missions.
- Returned ports should create Irish responsibilities, not only rewards.
- Delayed or failed settlement should raise British access pressure during war.

## Emergency, Coastwatching, and military weakness

The Emergency route should be active because Ireland needed coast watching, internal security, reserve forces, rationing, and defence preparation. The Look Out Post network provides a concrete basis for regional missions.

Useful sources:

- Military Archives material records 83 Look Out Posts around the coast.
- History Ireland describes the Coastwatching Service as Ireland's main marine and air intelligence gathering system during the Emergency and gives useful scale and role detail.
- Defence Forces history material records the wartime expansion of the Defence Forces and the Local Defence Force.

Design consequence:

- Coastwatching should be a mission family with regional targets.
- Emergency Preparedness should include watch posts, reserves, ports, supply, and air or sea warning.
- The player should need equipment, manpower, supply, and state targets instead of receiving repeated free units.

## Labour route

Labour should have a constitutional route and a possible radical line. William Norton provides a democratic Labour leadership anchor. Frank Ryan and the Irish International Brigade experience provide anti fascist route material, but exact quotes or slogans remain blocked until text research.

Useful sources:

- DIB and labour movement material on William Norton.
- DIB material on Frank Ryan and Irish International Brigade service.

Design consequence:

- Labour must govern through social legislation and cooperative economics, not only revolution.
- Worker defence committees should be legal and disciplined in the democratic path.
- Anti fascist foreign ties should create sponsor access pressure.

## Blueshirt route

The Blueshirt route should be authoritarian, costly, and separate from Fine Gael constitutional opposition. Eoin O'Duffy, the National Guard, Catholic corporatism, anti communism, and the Irish Brigade in Spain provide route anchors.

Useful sources:

- DIB entry on Eoin O'Duffy.
- History Ireland material on O'Duffy, Abyssinia, and international fascism.
- Documents on Irish Foreign Policy material on the Irish Brigade in Spain.

Design consequence:

- Fine Gael legalism must not automatically become Blueshirt rule.
- The Blueshirt route requires route locks and armed movement pressure.
- Foreign right wing ties should raise access pressure and reduce democratic legitimacy.

## IRA underground route and Plan Kathleen style risk

The IRA route should be an unstable underground path. German contact and Plan Kathleen style material should be dangerous and exposed to counter intelligence, not a clean alliance path.

Useful sources:

- National Archives UK material on Hermann Goertz and German aims associated with IRA assistance.
- DIB entry on Seán Russell.
- Prior research notes on Plan Kathleen style content.

Design consequence:

- Foreign courier decisions should have exposure timers.
- Taking German aid should damage Allied and United States support.
- The route can have powerful irregular tools, but it should carry splinter, crackdown, and diplomatic failure states.

## Industry and supply

Ireland's industry branch should use real economic anchors and regional projects. Electrification, state industry, sugar beet, turf, ports, and emergency stores are stronger than generic factory rewards.

Useful sources:

- DIB material on Seán Lemass and state industrial policy.
- RTÉ Archives material on Carlow sugar and the Irish Sugar Company.
- Bord na Móna material on the Turf Development Board and peat fuel development.

Design consequence:

- Industry decisions should target named projects and regions.
- Sugar beet and turf should affect emergency food and fuel security.
- Port repair and merchant registry decisions should connect trade and defence.

## Partition, Boundary Commission, and Northern settlement

The Northern route should combine legal memory, local support, British reaction, and unionist alarm. The Boundary Commission and the constitutional claim over the island provide historical anchors, but gameplay must handle international reactions and post settlement integration.

Useful sources:

- National Archives UK education material on the Boundary Commission.
- CAIN material on the 1937 constitutional articles relating to the island.
- Part 1 bibliography and historical notes on partition.

Design consequence:

- The border branch starts with documentation and support networks, not claims.
- Peaceful settlement needs local guarantees and observers.
- Violent routes should trigger British and unionist resistance.
- Unified Ireland must require post settlement integration.

## Mechanic presentation conclusion

Part 2 chooses decision category headers plus national spirit tooltips for the four mechanics. This keeps the first implementation tractable and puts values beside actions. A scripted GUI remains possible later only if the headers cannot show value causes and thresholds clearly.
