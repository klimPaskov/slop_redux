# Ireland focus tree research notes, part 3 addendum

This addendum supports AI and country package planning. It records source anchors used for route relevance and asset source mode. It does not provide final localisation.

## Constitutional and sovereignty anchors

The Constitution of Ireland source basis remains the official Irish Statute Book text, which states that the Constitution was enacted by the people on 1 July 1937 and came into operation on 29 December 1937. This supports the constitutional trunk, presidency handling, legal opposition route, and legitimacy mechanics.

The Treaty Ports source basis remains Documents on Irish Foreign Policy material on the 1938 transfer of harbour defences. This supports port return focuses, port garrison missions, and foreign access pressure.

## Emergency defence anchors

Irish Defence Forces historical material notes wartime mobilization and the Local Defence Force peak, which supports the LDF reserve pathway and Emergency Preparedness mechanic. Military Archives material on Look Out Post logbooks supports the coastwatching and G2 intelligence lane.

## Political actor anchors

Dictionary of Irish Biography entries for Éamon de Valera, Frank Aiken, Seán Lemass, Douglas Hyde, W. T. Cosgrave, William Norton, Eoin O'Duffy, James Craig, and J. M. Andrews support the leader and portrait source requirements.

Fine Gael legal opposition and Blueshirt corporatist content must remain separated because O'Duffy's role in the Blueshirt movement and the later conservative opposition route produce different institutions, risks, and AI behavior.

Labour route design uses William Norton and trade union politics as the democratic socialist anchor. Soviet contact remains a rare radical branch, not the default Labour identity.

## IRA and German contact anchors

National Archives UK material on Hermann Goertz and the Irish Story account of Plan Kathleen support the IRA foreign contact route as an intelligence and exposure risk. The route should never treat German contact as clean aid.

## Northern Ireland anchors

James Craig and J. M. Andrews source needs support Northern actor or tag planning when implementation represents Northern Ireland actively. Unionist alarm, British guarantee pressure, and local safeguards should be mechanics or state logic, not only focus effects.

- Real leader portraits need sourced historical images.
- Base Ireland flag should be preserved unless route identity changes through a deliberate cosmetic tag.
- Historical or attested Blueshirt, republican, Labour, Northern, or official symbols need source review before use.
