# Ireland focus tree spec, part 2 route architecture

## High level lane map

The focus tree should be arranged as a broad non linear tree with a shared opening trunk in the center, politics spreading across the upper half, industry and emergency defence as support lanes below, diplomacy on one side, and the Northern settlement lane on the other side. Hidden or rare content should be visually tucked behind route locks or revealed focus groups rather than placed in the open tree from game start.

Working layout guide:

| Lane | Role | Unlock timing | Route interaction |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Constitutional trunk | Establishes early state problems, first reforms, and route tension | Game start | Leads to all politics and first support choices |
| Sovereign neutrality | Historical de Valera path | After constitutional lock | Strengthens neutrality, Emergency, legal sovereignty, later arbitration |
| Constitutional opposition | Democratic opposition and guarded Commonwealth cooperation | After opposition lock | Adds Allied options, legal reforms, stronger parliament, possible defence pact |
| Labour social republic | Labour, trade unions, anti fascist volunteers, cooperative economy | After labour lock | Changes economy, militia model, foreign alignment, all island labour strategy |
| Blueshirt corporatist route | O'Duffy, National Guard, corporatist and anti communist path | After public order crisis | Militarizes politics, adds right wing foreign ties, increases internal opposition |
| IRA underground route | Illegal republican pressure, sabotage, border escalation, foreign intelligence risk | After underground crisis | Opens revolutionary tools, border conflict, German contacts, Army Council pressure |
| Industry and supply | Economic recovery, electrification, native industry, emergency stockpiles | Early and mid game | Route variants alter ownership and cost model |
| Military and coast defence | Army, reserves, coastwatching, ports, air corps, naval patrols | Early and mid game | Route variants alter force type and militia control |
| Diplomacy | Neutrality, Allied cooperation, Vatican, Washington, Berlin, Rome, Moscow | Mid game | Route locked foreign options and sponsor pressure |
| Northern settlement | Partition, border politics, plebiscites, war goals, integration | Mid and late game | Route identities determine peaceful, legal, socialist, corporatist, or underground method |
| Late game ambition | Postwar order, united Ireland, Atlantic role, hidden restoration if accepted | Late game | Converges successful routes into end states |

## Opening constitutional trunk

The opening trunk should make the player solve first state problems before choosing an ideology. It should not force one straight path. Early focuses should provide choices among constitutional legitimacy, economic relief, public order, and defence preparation.

Working focus groups:

| Working focus group | Purpose | Gameplay effect direction | Follow up |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Audit the Free State | Establish 1936 starting weaknesses | Reveals starting ideas and mechanic values | Opens economic, security, and constitutional starts |
| The Dáil Holds | Strengthen parliamentary authority | Raises Dáil legitimacy, lowers armed movement pressure | Unlocks legal party negotiations |
| Civil War Wounds | Address old factional mistrust | Opens reconciliation decisions and veteran integration missions | Reduces route unrest if completed early |
| Economic War Accounts | Prepare settlement with Britain or autarky | Opens trade decision family and export strain mission | Leads to ports and industry forks |
| Constitutional Drafting | Move toward the 1937 constitution | Raises sovereignty, opens president and institutions choices | Leads to route lock event or focus fork |
| Public Order Bill | Confront illegal armies and street movements | Choice between policing, compromise, or militia tolerance | Affects Blueshirt and IRA route access |
| Port Question | Bring Treaty Ports into the tree early | Creates British negotiation pressure | Leads to returned ports or failed talks branch |
| Emergency Skeleton | First mobilisation and supply audit | Starts Emergency preparedness value | Opens military and coast defence lane |

The trunk should have several cross links. A player who handles public order gently can keep Labour and constitutional opposition stronger. A player who tolerates militias can make IRA or Blueshirt paths easier. A player who rushes ports without economic preparation should face higher British demands or delayed transfer.

## Political route architecture

### Historical sovereign neutrality lane

Narrative role: de Valera consolidates sovereignty while avoiding formal alignment.

Mechanical role: high legitimacy, high neutrality control, strong Emergency preparedness, careful foreign pressure management.

Focus groups:

| Focus group | Function | Decision or mechanic hooks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bunreacht and sovereignty | Make the constitution a major branch milestone | Raises Dáil legitimacy, changes country name and institutions where appropriate |
| Presidency and symbols | Install visible state identity and president related content | Leader or national spirit handling, source portrait needs |
| End the economic war | Reach British Irish settlement | Removes economic strain, modifies trade dependence, leads to ports |
| Return of the ports | Transfer Cobh, Berehaven, and Lough Swilly | Removes Treaty Ports idea, unlocks port defence decisions |
| Neutrality by preparation | Build ability to refuse pressure | Raises Emergency preparedness and neutral credibility |
| G2 and the watch posts | Intelligence and coast watching | Opens coastal observation missions and foreign agent incidents |
| The Emergency cabinet | Wartime governance | Balances emergency executive reach with legitimacy |
| Plan W without alliance | Limited contingency cooperation with Britain | Gives invasion response tools without joining faction unless route changes |
| Postwar arbitration | Slow reunification approach | Opens international settlement decisions if conditions permit |

Historical route payoffs should include a strong neutral defence package, improved economy, and a postwar diplomatic path. The route should be hard because the player must keep preparedness, legitimacy, and neutral credibility high at the same time.

### Constitutional opposition lane

Narrative role: parliamentary opposition returns to government and argues that sovereignty is best preserved through legal institutions and guarded cooperation with Britain and the Commonwealth.

Mechanical role: lowers armed movement pressure, increases Allied trust, strengthens industry through trade, and creates options for negotiated defence access.

Focus groups:

| Focus group | Function | Decision or mechanic hooks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rebuild the opposition benches | Strengthens Fine Gael and Cumann na nGaedheal veterans | Party popularity, advisor unlocks, parliamentary legitimacy |
| Parliamentary restraint | Reduces executive emergency drift | Limits emergency powers, improves democratic legitimacy |
| Commonwealth channel | Opens London and dominion diplomacy | British trust, trade decisions, possible defence talks |
| Defensive cooperation | Allows limited Allied naval and air coordination | Access pressure system, port decisions, neutrality tradeoff |
| Treaty review office | Uses legal framework for Northern questions | Border commission and arbitration decisions |
| Atlantic guarantee | Late route pact or Allied association | Requires war state and high British trust |
| An all island settlement | Late democratic reunification plan | Plebiscite, arbitration, and integration missions |

This route should not automatically join the Allies. It should let the player choose between strict democratic neutrality, limited cooperation, or full Allied association if Britain is under major threat or Ireland has gained enough concessions.

### Labour and social republic lane

Narrative role: Labour, trade unions, cooperative economics, and anti fascist veterans push Ireland toward a social republic.

Mechanical role: stronger mobilisation and industry through worker boards, lower church and employer support, better anti fascist foreign ties, and alternate reunification through class politics.

Focus groups:

| Focus group | Function | Decision or mechanic hooks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Labour in the Dáil | Brings Labour into the center of politics | Party popularity, coalition decisions, parliamentary legitimacy |
| Trade union councils | Creates economic and political support base | Worker board decisions, strikes or factory output tradeoff |
| Cooperative industry | Builds state backed industry and rural cooperatives | Factories, infrastructure, supply, sugar beet, turf programs |
| Veterans of Spain | Anti fascist military and symbolic branch | Frank Ryan or volunteer advisors, foreign volunteer memory |
| Workers defence committees | Controlled militia or reserve force | Unit decisions with equipment and legitimacy cost |
| Social republic | Route lock or capstone | Ruling party change, country identity, social legislation |
| All island labour | Northern labour and trade union pressure | Northern decisions, unionist reaction, British suspicion |
| International anti fascism | External alignment choice | Comintern sympathy, democratic anti fascism, or non aligned labour route |

The route should include a democratic socialist path and a more radical alignment path. It should not force the player into Soviet dependence unless they choose that branch and accept foreign access pressure.

### Blueshirt corporatist lane

Narrative role: O'Duffy and the militant right claim that the Dáil cannot defend the nation against communism, disorder, and partition.

Mechanical role: strong command, militia mobilisation, anti communist foreign ties, lower legitimacy, higher unrest, and aggressive Northern policy.

Focus groups:

| Focus group | Function | Decision or mechanic hooks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| National Guard revival | Rebuilds paramilitary right | Raises armed movement pressure, unlocks Blueshirt cadres |
| O'Duffy returns | Leader route fork | Leader change and portrait sourcing |
| Corporate chambers | Replaces parliamentary pluralism | National spirit transformation, law changes |
| Catholic corporatist diplomacy | Vatican, Spain, Italy, and anti communist ties | Foreign access pressure and volunteer decisions |
| Irish Brigade lessons | Military route from Spanish experience | Special unit templates, officer corps, anti communist missions |
| Discipline the unions | Internal crackdown | Raises command obedience, lowers labour support, risk of strikes |
| Northern crusade | Expansion branch | Border incidents, ultimatums, high British reaction |
| National Corporate State | Capstone | Country identity and late route mechanic |

The route must be strong but costly. It should not be treated as a harmless stability path. It should create risks of labour resistance, republican resistance, diplomatic isolation, and overmilitarized governance.

### IRA underground lane

Narrative role: illegal republican forces reject constitutional caution and use sabotage, border pressure, and foreign enemies of Britain to seek reunification.

Mechanical role: fast escalation, irregular units, intelligence exposure, German contact risk, and a crisis between civil and army authority.

Focus groups:

| Focus group | Function | Decision or mechanic hooks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Army Council speaks | Opens underground route | Lowers Dáil legitimacy, raises armed movement pressure |
| Absorb the underground | State tries to control IRA pressure | Hybrid route with lower chaos, limited irregulars |
| Supplant the Dáil | Army Council dominates politics | Hard route lock, severe diplomatic penalties |
| Border columns | Northern insurgent preparation | Irregular unit decisions and border missions |
| Sabotage network | S-Plan inspired tools | Covert operations with intelligence exposure and civilian backlash |
| Foreign courier | German contact risk | Plan Kathleen style incidents, foreign access pressure |
| Ulster operation | Violent reunification attempt | War goals or uprising missions with high failure consequences |
| Revolutionary republic | Capstone | Country identity, Army Council pressure, route events |

The route should include a safer absorption path and a dangerous underground takeover path. The dangerous path can become powerful in a world where Britain is collapsing, but it should be extremely risky if Britain remains strong.

## Industry and economy architecture

The industry lane should be split into early recovery, state industry, rural resources, port economy, and emergency supply. It should not be a simple factory ladder.

Focus groups:

| Focus group | Historical basis | Gameplay direction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tariff scars | Economic war recovery | Modify trade dependence and rural distress |
| Native manufactures | Protectionist state industry | Civilian and military factory projects with consumer goods costs |
| Shannon power | Ardnacrusha and grid development | Infrastructure, power themed industrial capacity, research bonus |
| Sugar beet belt | Irish Sugar Company and beet factories | Factories or food supply decisions in Carlow, Tuam, Mallow, Thurles style regions |
| Turf and bog development | Emergency fuel and later Bord na Móna direction | Fuel, construction, and resource decisions with efficiency tradeoffs |
| Port led recovery | Cork, Dublin, Waterford, Limerick, western ports | Dockyards, naval repair, trade, convoy and port decisions |
| Emergency stores | Wartime rationing and stockpiles | Timed stockpile missions and supply resilience |
| Rural works | Roads, rail, drainage, local employment | Infrastructure and building slot projects |

Route variants:

- Historical route favors state managed self sufficiency and emergency supply.
- Opposition route favors trade reopening and British market access.
- Labour route favors cooperative boards and worker committees.
- Blueshirt route favors corporatist chambers and discipline costs.
- IRA route favors clandestine supply and captured depots.

## Military and emergency defence architecture

The military lane should answer how a small state defends a long coastline and a contested border. It should include regular forces, local forces, intelligence, air observation, naval patrols, and route specific units.

Focus groups:

| Focus group | Gameplay role | Requirements and costs direction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Army establishment review | Fix command and templates | Army XP and officer decisions |
| Regular battalions | Improve professional army | Infantry equipment and manpower costs |
| Local Defence Force | Reserve and local defence | Low equipment cost, high local support requirement |
| Coastwatching network | Watch posts and intelligence | Support equipment, radio, coastal state missions |
| Port defence commands | Defend returned ports | Forts, coastal forts, anti air, garrison missions |
| Air Corps expansion | Recon and limited defence | Air XP, aircraft, airbase construction |
| Naval service foundations | Patrol and minesweeping style role | Convoys, dockyard time, coastal missions |
| G2 intelligence | Counter espionage and foreign agents | Political legitimacy and intelligence exposure logic |
| Plan W contingency | Defensive cooperation if allowed | British trust or route condition, no automatic alliance |
| Route militias | Blueshirt, worker, IRA, or constitutional reserves | Route specific costs and risks |

## Diplomacy architecture

Ireland's diplomacy should be a balancing game. It should not be a single faction join line.

Focus groups:

| Focus group | Route access | Gameplay role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| League voice | Broad access | Diplomatic recognition and arbitration tools |
| London channel | Historical and opposition | Trade settlement, ports, defence pressure |
| Washington and diaspora | Historical, Labour, opposition | American support, recognition, aid, later war pressure |
| Vatican channel | Historical, Blueshirt, opposition | Catholic legitimacy, conservative support, social policy pressure |
| Berlin contact | IRA, rare historical crisis | Intelligence risk, German aid, Plan Kathleen incidents |
| Rome and Burgos memory | Blueshirt | Anti communist diplomacy and volunteer legacy |
| Moscow and anti fascist networks | Labour radical | Anti fascist aid, Soviet dependence risk |
| Atlantic neutrality | Historical capstone | Neutral credibility and pressure management |
| Allied association | Opposition or late historical emergency | Faction or access choice, with partition bargaining |

## Northern settlement architecture

The Northern lane must be visible and distinct. It should unlock only after the player has chosen a political route or after partition pressure reaches a threshold.

Shared early focus groups:

| Focus group | Function | Unlocks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Border survey | Names the contested problem | State group tooltips, partition pressure meter |
| Nationalist committees | Build peaceful networks | Local support decisions and British suspicion |
| Unionist alarm | Represents reaction | AI and event pressure, not a player reward |
| Border commission revived | Legal route | Arbitration and claims without war |
| Plebiscite preparation | Democratic route | Timed missions with legitimacy and foreign observers |
| Frontier defence | Military route | Border garrison missions, forts, mobilisation |
| Ulster settlement office | Post victory or post deal | Integration missions, compliance, resistance control |

Route variants:

- Historical route seeks delayed international arbitration and postwar settlement.
- Opposition route trades defence cooperation for boundary or plebiscite concessions.
- Labour route organizes cross border worker and tenant networks.
- Blueshirt route escalates confrontation and seeks a coercive settlement.
- IRA route builds underground columns and foreign backed operations.

## Late game route endings

Each route needs a late payoff that changes play rather than simply ending the branch.

| Route | Late payoff direction | Failure state |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sovereign neutrality | Neutral Ireland with high preparedness and a postwar settlement route | Foreign pressure overwhelms neutrality or partition remains frozen |
| Opposition | Allied associated or Commonwealth linked democratic Ireland with settlement leverage | Ireland becomes dependent without gaining Northern concessions |
| Labour | Social republic with cooperative economy and all island labour legitimacy | Church, employer, or foreign pressure fractures the coalition |
| Blueshirt | National Corporate State with militarized anti communist agenda | Strikes, republican resistance, and isolation weaken the state |
| IRA | Revolutionary republic or Army Council state with border escalation tools | Intelligence exposure, German dependency, or failed uprising causes crackdown |
| Hidden cultural restoration if accepted | Symbolic Gaelic state identity with rare late formable or ceremonial branch | Public legitimacy collapses if treated as normal politics |

## Focus filters and search categories

The implementation should add or reuse focus filters so the player can find the route logic.

Recommended filter categories:

- Constitutional and politics
- Emergency defence
- Neutrality and diplomacy
- Industry and supply
- Northern settlement
- Military and reserves
- Route locked
- Hidden or revealed content

These filters are implementation handles, not player facing text drafts.
