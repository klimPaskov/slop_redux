# Ireland focus tree spec, part 3 historical route notes

## Research purpose

This section records the historical connections already accepted as design anchors. It is not a full bibliography and it is not final localisation. The next parts should use these anchors to keep alternate history plausible and regionally specific.

## Constitutional sovereignty

The 1937 constitution should be a central focus milestone. It gives the historical route a state identity payoff and gives other routes something to accept, amend, replace, or undermine. The route should treat the constitution as a political settlement with visible institutions and tensions. It should not be a small stability focus.

Design uses:

- Establish the country identity of Éire or Ireland depending route handling.
- Add president and constitutional institutions.
- Raise Dáil legitimacy if passed through constitutional means.
- Create alternate route choices for Labour amendments, opposition legal reform, corporatist replacement, or Army Council rejection.
- Unlock constitutional arbitration language for the Northern settlement lane.

## Treaty Ports and the 1938 settlement

The return of Cobh, Berehaven, and Lough Swilly is the most important defence and neutrality anchor. The ports should begin as a starting national spirit or foreign pressure state. A historical player should regain them through settlement with Britain. Alternate players can trade, delay, militarize, or mishandle the issue.

Design uses:

- Starting idea: Treaty Ports in Foreign Hands.
- Focus chain: economic war settlement, British Irish agreement, port transfer, port defence command.
- Decision family: port fortification, naval stores, coastwatch stations, local garrisons, convoy patrols.
- Diplomacy pressure: Britain will value access if war begins.
- Failure: if ports remain outside Irish control during a world war, neutral credibility and defence preparedness suffer.

## The Emergency and coast watching

The Emergency should be a living defensive system. The Coastwatching Service and the network of Look Out Posts offer a perfect mission family for a small country. They give Ireland useful intelligence without creating a large navy. The player should build the network, staff posts, improve communication, and react to incidents at sea and in the air.

Design uses:

- Emergency preparedness value.
- Coastal state missions for watch posts.
- G2 intelligence and counter espionage decisions.
- British and German incident events.
- Local Defence Force recruitment that depends on public legitimacy and equipment.
- Route variants for constitutional reserve, worker defence, Blueshirt cadres, or IRA columns.

## Economic sovereignty and state industry

Ireland's industrial route should focus on state building and self sufficiency. The Shannon Scheme, sugar beet, native manufacturing, turf development, ports, fisheries, and emergency supply can make an economy lane that is specific to Ireland. This should replace generic civilian factory chains.

Design uses:

- Shannon and grid development as infrastructure and industrial capacity.
- Sugar beet and rural factories as food supply, local employment, and state industry.
- Turf and bog development as emergency fuel, with efficiency limits and labour costs.
- Port economies for dockyards, convoy capacity, and trade.
- Native manufacture decisions for small arms and support equipment.

## Blueshirts, Fine Gael, and the right wing split

The tree must separate the democratic opposition route from the Blueshirt corporatist route. Fine Gael and Cumann na nGaedheal should support a constitutional alternative. Eoin O'Duffy and the militant National Guard legacy should be a route that requires deliberate empowerment and public order failures.

Design uses:

- Opposition democratic route can be led by constitutional conservatives.
- Blueshirt route requires militia tolerance, public order crisis, or radical right choices.
- O'Duffy route creates anti communist and foreign right wing links.
- Spain and Italy should shape route flavor and volunteer related mechanics.
- Internal opposition should increase if the route dismantles parliamentary checks.

## Labour, Connolly memory, and anti fascism

Labour should not be a thin communist branch. It should have democratic socialist, trade union, and cooperative economic options. Frank Ryan and International Brigade memory can support an anti fascist military or cultural branch, but the route should not turn every Labour path into Soviet dependence.

Design uses:

- Worker councils and cooperative boards for industry.
- Labour coalition decisions that can stay democratic.
- Anti fascist volunteer legacy for advisors or limited unit concepts.
- Radical foreign alignment only by choice.
- Church and employer resistance as internal challenges.
- Northern labour solidarity as a non military route to all island pressure.

## IRA, S-Plan, Plan Kathleen, and underground pressure

The IRA route should be high risk and historically grounded in sabotage, partition pressure, and foreign intelligence attempts. Plan Kathleen should be a warning anchor for the route, not a magical invasion button. German support should bring intelligence exposure, diplomatic collapse, and loss of civil authority.

Design uses:

- Army Council pressure as a mechanic component.
- Underground route split between absorption and takeover.
- Sabotage decisions with real costs, cooldowns, and civilian backlash.
- Border column missions that require equipment, local support, and safehouses.
- German contact incidents that can provide aid but damage legitimacy.
- Failure state where the state crackdown or British counter pressure weakens the route.

## Partition as an active but restrained system

Northern Ireland should not be treated as a passive target. Britain, the Northern government, unionist communities, nationalist networks, and the global war state should all matter. Peaceful routes need time and foreign legitimacy. Violent routes need equipment and risk. Post settlement integration should be required before full benefit.

Design uses:

- Partition pressure value.
- Border state group tooltips.
- Plebiscite, arbitration, and international observer missions.
- Border defence and underground missions.
- British and Northern AI reactions.
- Integration projects after victory or settlement.
- No instant full cores across large contested territory without work.

## Accepted alternate history boundaries

The following alternate paths are accepted for detailed expansion in the next parts:

- Historical sovereign neutrality with active Emergency play.
- Democratic constitutional opposition with possible Allied or Commonwealth cooperation.
- Labour social republic with democratic and radical sub branches.
- Blueshirt corporatist Ireland as a route locked, costly, authoritarian path.
- IRA underground republic with absorption and takeover branches.
- Multiple reunification routes tied to legitimacy, pressure, war state, foreign access, and state control.

The following earlier review ideas have been resolved in later files:

- Civic cultural restoration is accepted as a rare hidden constitutional and cultural path in part 10. Symbolic high kingship is not accepted as a route payoff.
- A rare Atlantic compact is accepted as a conference or limited faction concept, not a formable country.
- A second stage all island formable beyond ordinary unified Ireland remains rejected by default unless a later accepted addendum proves a concrete gameplay need.

## Alternate history boundaries already established

- Do not make Fine Gael identical to the Blueshirts.
- Do not make the IRA route a clean German alliance.
- Do not make Labour only a Soviet branch.
- Do not make de Valera's historical path a passive neutrality branch.
- Make the ports settlement a real gameplay settlement with events, decisions, and defence work.
- Do not make the Northern route a list of claims.
- Do not make industry generic factories without Irish state building themes.
- Do not add occult or mythical content unless a later planning pass proves that it adds playable value and can remain clearly rare.
