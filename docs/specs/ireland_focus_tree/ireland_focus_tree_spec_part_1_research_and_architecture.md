# Ireland Gameplay Overhaul and Focus Tree

## Part 1: Historical Foundation and Overall Gameplay Architecture

Feature slug: `ireland_focus_tree`

All route names, focus group names, idea names, mechanic labels, and event family labels in this document are working labels and are not final localisation.

Historical status markers:

- `H` documented history
- `P` plausible speculation
- `A` deliberate absurdity

## Feature promise

Ireland should begin as a small country with large political claims, limited material means, and several different definitions of independence competing inside the same state. The player should not spend the opening years waiting for a world war. The player should build a constitution, decide how the state remembers the revolution, settle or prolong the Economic War, prepare the returned Treaty Ports, manage church and labour influence, contain or reconcile militant republicanism, and decide what neutrality will require in practice.

The campaign should turn the Second World War into an Irish political and strategic emergency even when Ireland remains formally neutral. Ships disappear. Fuel and imported machinery become scarce. Foreign aircraft cross Irish skies. Belfast burns. Intelligence services test Irish institutions. Republican militants see opportunity. Britain seeks access. Germany seeks disruption. The United States becomes harder to ignore. The government can preserve neutrality only by making repeated choices that change defence, trade, internal legitimacy, and relations with both belligerent camps.

The overhaul must support several outcomes without treating them as interchangeable ideology skins:

- a durable constitutional republic that proves active neutrality can be sovereign policy
- a modernising developmental republic built around industry, state capacity, and pragmatic diplomacy
- a Fine Gael constitutional government that restores trust in law, defence, and external cooperation
- a democratic pro-Allied intervention that overcomes the political consensus around neutrality
- a Labour-led social republic that changes welfare, labour power, economic ownership, and coalition politics
- a reconstructed socialist republican movement that links national and class revolution
- an IRA-led revolutionary state that must convert underground authority into government and military command
- a corporatist seizure rooted in O'Duffy's movement, anti-communism, veterans, and emergency breakdown
- a Catholic vocational order that grows through real social institutions and constitutional compromise
- a hidden Ailtirí takeover that emerges from wartime radicalisation and coercive Gaelic totalitarianism
- a serious Gaelic cultural renaissance that can support several constitutional routes
- difficult hidden absurd restorations built from mythology, old institutions, provincial identity, folklore, and unusual campaign conditions

Each result must be visible through leaders, parties, laws, decisions, national spirits, military structures, economic policy, diplomacy, flags, country names, events, AI behavior, and late-game goals.

## The player's three campaign questions

### Who owns the republic?

The state claims democratic authority. Militant republicans claim that the revolutionary mandate remains unfinished. Fianna Fáil claims to have brought republicanism into the institutions. Fine Gael claims that the institutions survived because the pro-Treaty state defended constitutional order. Labour claims that political independence remains incomplete without social change. Church leaders claim authority over the moral and social foundation of the nation. Veterans, Garda officers, soldiers, civil servants, local organisations, and families retain different memories of the Civil War.

This question drives the central internal struggle.

### How can independence be defended?

Ireland can mobilise people, but it lacks equipment, shipping, fuel, air defence, naval strength, and industrial depth. The returned ports create opportunities and vulnerabilities. Britain can provide arms and operational support while demanding access. The United States can provide equipment and diplomatic leverage while pressing Allied interests. Germany can promise anti-British opportunity while offering unreliable support and severe danger. Independent defence requires years of preparation and sustained economic sacrifice.

This question drives the military, economic, intelligence, and diplomatic systems.

### What is the national project for?

Independence can protect parliamentary government, complete national unity, build a social republic, preserve Catholic social order, revive Irish language and culture, create an industrial state, or launch a revolutionary transformation. The player must choose how these aims rank when they conflict.

This question drives route identity and late-game conclusions.

## Opening situation in January 1936

Ireland begins before the 1937 Constitution and before the 1938 settlement with Britain. The opening state is politically functional but strategically unsettled.

### Political position

- Fianna Fáil governs under Éamon de Valera.
- Fine Gael remains the principal constitutional opposition under W. T. Cosgrave.
- Labour can influence parliamentary outcomes but does not command the state.
- The IRA retains clandestine networks and claims revolutionary legitimacy.
- Republican Congress veterans and socialist republicans remain fragmented.
- Eoin O'Duffy's corporatist movement is marginal but not irrelevant.
- Catholic institutions possess broad social authority without forming a single party command.
- the constitutional position of the state, its external links, and the territorial claim are still being revised

### Economic position

- the Economic War continues
- agricultural exporters face pressure
- protection supports some domestic industry
- the state lacks deep industrial, transport, shipping, and energy capacity
- imported machinery, fuel, and manufactured inputs remain strategically important
- unemployment, underemployment, rural poverty, and emigration shape politics

### Military position

- the permanent army is small
- equipment and communications are inadequate for modern war
- maritime defence is minimal
- air defence is weak
- the British still control the Treaty Ports
- defence planning must account for British, German, and internal threats

### Social position

- the Civil War split remains present in party and institutional memory
- Catholic social influence is strong
- organised labour seeks wages, recognition, and reform
- Irish language revival has constitutional and cultural importance but limited administrative reach
- regional differences affect political support, economic needs, and security

### National position

- the state governs twenty-six counties
- Northern Ireland remains within the United Kingdom
- the constitutional and emotional claim to the whole island remains central
- southern policy toward northern nationalists, unionists, labour, and the British government is unresolved

## Opening player experience

The first two years should contain competing deadlines and opportunities. The player cannot complete every project before the European crisis.

### Opening pressure set

1. prepare the constitutional settlement
2. decide whether to settle or intensify the Economic War
3. manage public order without strengthening the IRA through excessive repression
4. decide how much influence church organisations receive in education and social policy
5. respond to labour demands and industrial disputes
6. professionalise the army or build a broader territorial defence system
7. prepare a position on the Treaty Ports
8. choose whether Irish-language policy emphasises administration, education, regional development, or symbolic nationalism
9. establish policy toward Northern Ireland and border organisations
10. decide whether foreign contacts remain diplomatic, commercial, military, or clandestine

The opening should create early specialisation. A player who spends heavily on coastal defence enters 1939 with less industrial capacity. A player who forces industrialisation may lack rural support and imported machinery. A player who reconciles republicans may weaken immediate state authority but gain auxiliary defence networks. A player who represses every militant group may stabilise the cabinet while making a later insurrection more severe.

## Design pillars

### One shared national crisis

All routes begin from the same constitutional, economic, military, and partition problems. Alternate history is created by different answers to those problems. No major route receives a detached fantasy opening that ignores the 1936 state.

### Neutrality is active gameplay

Neutrality requires patrols, internment policy, trade negotiation, censorship, intelligence choices, coastal observation, airspace responses, shipping, rescue, weather reporting, and parliamentary legitimacy. A neutral Ireland should make frequent consequential decisions.

### The Civil War remains politically alive

The old split affects leaders, institutions, local support, military obedience, security choices, and the language of legitimacy. It can be reconciled, transformed, exploited, or reopened.

### Northern Ireland is a society

Northern Ireland contains unionist government, a Protestant majority, a nationalist minority, industrial labour, strategic ports, British forces, local security institutions, and contested local government. Unity requires politics, security, administration, and integration.

### Economic policy supports strategy

Agriculture, industry, turf, electricity, rail, ports, shipping, and foreign trade are not a generic factory branch. Each economic route supports a different defence and diplomatic model.

### Military weakness produces choices

Ireland cannot become a normal major power through several free military factories. The player chooses between a professional army, territorial mobilisation, guerrilla integration, Allied interoperability, corporatist militarisation, and hidden extreme systems.

### Social power is cross-route

Church, labour, cultural organisations, veterans, civil servants, and regional networks influence several routes. They are not limited to one isolated political branch.

### Events carry the country

Events are the primary connective tissue among history, route choices, values, decisions, and foreign reactions. Flavour events must affect play and provide context.

### Absurdity is earned

Hidden impossible routes require real cultural work, unusual world conditions, route-compatible decisions, and sustained discovery. A mythic outcome should feel like the country has reinterpreted every normal institution through an impossible claim.

## Core gameplay loop

The national overhaul uses a repeated five-step loop.

### 1. Establish policy

Focuses, elections, cabinet choices, laws, and major events establish the public policy of the state.

Examples:

- constitutional settlement
- trade settlement or escalation
- neutrality doctrine
- labour arbitration model
- church relationship
- military organisation
- language policy
- Northern Ireland strategy

### 2. Commit resources

Decisions and missions convert policy into action.

Examples:

- deploy supplied units to named ports
- build coastwatch links
- assign civilian factories to emergency imports
- charter merchant vessels
- supply border Garda posts
- hold a public vote or negotiate a coalition
- integrate local defence volunteers
- deliver aid after a bombing raid
- establish a foreign liaison channel

### 3. Move the national values

Every important action affects the central internal struggle and one or more companion values. Visible changes explain why routes open, close, or enter crisis.

### 4. Absorb events and external pressure

Historical and dynamic events test the policy. The same event has different choices and effects under different routes.

Examples:

- a foreign aircraft landing
- an IRA arms cache
- a bishops' statement
- a shipping loss
- an industrial strike
- a British request
- a German intelligence approach
- a Northern Ireland crisis
- a local language dispute

### 5. Resolve, transform, or fail

Success upgrades institutions and expands options. Partial success produces mixed results. Failure changes the route rather than merely applying a small penalty.

Examples:

- a failed crackdown creates martyrs and a harder security mission
- an incomplete defence project protects one port and leaves another exposed
- a trade deal secures fuel but increases foreign leverage
- a unity conference creates a federal transition instead of immediate annexation
- a Labour coalition passes welfare reform but loses control of militant unions

## The central internal struggle

Working mechanic label: `The National Settlement`

This label describes the struggle over which authority can define the state and complete the national project. Part 2 will determine final presentation and exact tuning.

### Initial poles

#### Constitutional State Authority

Sources of power:

- Dáil and cabinet legitimacy
- courts and civil service
- Garda and army obedience
- functioning elections
- constitutional settlement
- administrative delivery
- control of emergency powers

Strengths:

- reliable laws and administration
- foreign credibility
- easier economic planning
- stronger command and decision cleanup

Risks at extreme dominance:

- executive overreach
- censorship and detention abuse
- loss of republican legitimacy
- labour and regional alienation
- institutional rigidity

#### Unfinished Revolutionary Mandate

Sources of power:

- anti-Treaty memory
- IRA organisation
- partition grievance
- republican veterans
- local militant networks
- revolutionary commemorations
- perceived compromise with Britain

Strengths:

- anti-invasion mobilisation
- clandestine intelligence
- border networks
- political pressure for unity
- access to radical routes

Risks at extreme dominance:

- armed challenges to government
- factional violence
- foreign subversion
- damaged trade and diplomacy
- fragmented command

### Balanced zone

A balanced settlement is not always the safest state. It allows reconciliation and broad legitimacy, but it also permits rival organisations to survive. Some routes need a balanced zone to hold elections, negotiate an amnesty, build coalition government, or integrate republican auxiliaries. Prolonged balance without policy can trigger institutional paralysis.

### Companion values

The core balance cannot carry every function. Three companion values provide readable campaign pressure.

#### Social Consent

Represents willingness of citizens and organised groups to accept the current state direction.

Contributors:

- election results
- church sanction or opposition
- Labour and union support
- rural prices and food policy
- minority guarantees
- censorship and repression
- casualties and shortages
- successful local administration

Uses:

- coalition stability
- mobilisation
- emergency law
- integration of Northern Ireland
- radical route support
- resistance to coups

#### National Readiness

Represents the combined ability to defend territory and sustain the Emergency.

Contributors:

- equipment and trained forces
- ports and coastal observation
- air defence
- communications
- merchant shipping
- emergency stocks
- infrastructure and fuel
- operational planning

Uses:

- invasion outcomes
- foreign bargaining
- neutrality credibility
- war-entry options
- crisis mission difficulty

#### External Leverage

Represents how much foreign governments can shape Irish choices through aid, trade, access, intelligence, debt, or security dependence.

The value needs a breakdown by source rather than one anonymous number. At minimum the later design should track British, American, German, and Catholic diplomatic influence where relevant.

Uses:

- aid terms
- diplomatic options
- puppet or dependency risk
- intelligence events
- unity bargaining
- route disqualification

### Stakeholder contributions

The player should see why the balance moved. The mechanic breakdown should name at least these contributors when active:

- cabinet and Dáil
- Defence Forces command
- Garda and security administration
- IRA and militant republican networks
- Labour and trade unions
- Catholic hierarchy and social institutions
- local government and rural organisations
- northern nationalist contacts
- foreign leverage

Not every contributor needs a separate bar. They can appear as dynamic breakdown entries, event targets, decision modifiers, and route-specific thresholds.

1. cabinet legitimacy crisis
2. military obedience crisis
3. IRA campaign and detention crisis

5. church-state confrontation

## Starting national spirit architecture

The overhaul should begin with a small number of deep spirits. Working labels are not final localisation.

| Working spirit | Starting role | Main problems | Lifecycle direction |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `The Unfinished Republic` | mixed political identity | Civil War memory, partition, rival legitimacy | reconciled constitutional settlement, hardened state, revolutionary mandate, federal settlement, or hidden restoration |
| `A Rural Economy Under Strain` | economic weakness and social base | export dependence, low industrial depth, unemployment, imported inputs | agrarian resilience, developmental state, social economy, corporate economy, war dependency, or extreme autarky |
| `Defence Without Means` | military and strategic weakness | equipment, air defence, maritime control, communications, readiness | professional defence, territorial defence, Allied interoperability, revolutionary army, corporate mobilisation, or hidden host system |

The National Settlement is a mechanic, not another passive spirit. Church power, labour power, and neutrality policy should appear through the mechanic, laws, dynamic modifiers, decisions, and route stages instead of creating a large starting stack.

A fourth long-term route spirit can appear only by replacing or merging one of the three core spirits. Temporary emergency modifiers and unrelated global systems are separate.

## Focus-tree macro architecture

The tree should be visually broad, with a shared opening centre and several interacting lanes. The political branches should occupy the upper middle. Economy and society should extend to one side, defence and diplomacy to the other, and the Northern Ireland and late-game lanes should form a lower convergence layer.

### Shared opening centre

The opening centre establishes the state problems and creates several early choices before a permanent route lock.

Focus groups:

1. government and constitutional preparation
2. Civil War memory and public order
3. Economic War and trade position
4. army estimates and defence review
5. church, labour, and education consultation
6. border and Northern Ireland policy review
7. Irish-language and cultural administration

Expected shape:

- two or three short shared entry focuses
- several optional side branches
- early choices that affect values and later costs
- no immediate ideology lock
- at least one convergence before the 1937 constitutional decision

### 1937 constitutional gateway

The constitutional gateway is the first major political sorting point. It should permit different outcomes while preserving parliamentary play unless the player has already created a severe crisis.

Possible public choices:

- de Valera's constitutional republic
- a more cautious legal settlement shaped by opposition bargaining
- stronger vocational and Catholic social provisions
- stronger labour and social guarantees
- a delayed or failed settlement that increases crisis pressure

The gateway affects:

- state name and constitutional identity
- president and executive authority
- war powers and emergency law
- territorial claim language
- religious and minority settlement
- language policy
- the National Settlement balance
- later route access

A failed constitutional process should create a playable crisis route, not a dead campaign.

### 1938 sovereignty and trade gateway

The second shared gateway combines the Economic War settlement and return of the Treaty Ports.

Possible approaches:

- negotiated settlement and port transfer
- delayed settlement for stronger terms
- continued economic confrontation
- opposition-led trade normalisation
- secret military conditions attached to the transfer
- radical rejection of any agreement

The gateway affects:

- British relations
- agricultural exports
- industrial protection
- port ownership and buildings
- coastal defence missions
- National Readiness
- foreign leverage
- neutrality credibility

### Political route crown

After the constitutional and sovereignty gateways, the political tree opens into five visible route families and several hidden transformations.

1. constitutional republican government
2. constitutional opposition and coalition government
3. labour and social republic
4. revolutionary republican rupture
5. corporate and vocational order

Hidden transformations can emerge later from route states:

- Ailtirí wartime takeover
- mythic or old-institution restorations
- rare emergency military or presidential outcomes

### Shared support lanes

The support lanes remain partly available across routes but change their decisions, rewards, and events.

1. economy and national development
2. defence and mobilisation
3. maritime and air security
4. diplomacy and neutrality
5. social policy, labour, church, language, and education
6. Northern Ireland and border policy
7. intelligence and internal security

### Late-game convergence layer

Every major political family must connect to a late-game conclusion that combines:

- government settlement
- economic model
- military model
- foreign alignment or neutrality outcome
- Northern Ireland policy
- postwar emergency settlement
- country identity

The same political route can have more than one conclusion when support branches produce different strategic outcomes.

These are architecture summaries only. Parts 2 and 3 will provide full route maps.

### Family A: Constitutional republican government

Historical basis: `H`

Starting leadership:

- Éamon de Valera
- Fianna Fáil government

1. sovereign constitutional neutrality
2. Lemass developmental acceleration
3. republican reconciliation under state authority

Core gameplay:

- complete the constitutional settlement

## Hidden cultural and absurd architecture

The hidden layer should be invisible as a normal political branch. The player discovers it by combining cultural work, regional conditions, event choices, and unusual campaign states.

### Discovery tiers

#### Tier 1: documented cultural work

Classification: `H`

- folklore collection
- language development
- historic site surveys
- regional festivals
- manuscript and oral tradition projects
- pan-Celtic cultural exchange

These actions are useful to normal routes and do not reveal absurd content by themselves.

#### Tier 2: antiquarian political reinterpretation

Classification: `P`

- symbolic provincial councils
- Brehon law commissions
- Tara or Meath constitutional symbolism
- revived ceremonial offices
- Fianna-inspired defence organisations

These actions can remain serious cultural politics or begin the hidden route.

#### Tier 3: impossible confirmation

Classification: `A`

- events at legendary sites
- impossible weather or omens
- artefacts or figures that cannot be explained by normal politics
- provincial or mythological claims gaining material force

The player should not see the full route until this threshold is crossed.

#### Tier 4: restoration conflict

Classification: `A`

Possible families:

- High Kingship and the fifth province
- restored Fianna and Fenian order
- Tuatha Dé Danann sovereignty
- Aos Sí compact or hostile awakening
- rival provincial restoration
- pan-Celtic impossible federation

Each family must have its own government model, units, decisions, events, foreign reactions, AI restrictions, assets, and victory condition.

### Hidden-route rules

1. normal AI cannot enter without a rare designated profile and valid conditions
2. the route cannot be revealed by reading ordinary focus descriptions
3. serious cultural policy remains valuable when the route never appears
4. route failure can return the country to normal politics with lasting consequences
5. full success can become intentionally overpowered because discovery and completion are campaign-scale achievements
6. no hidden route can consist only of claims and combat bonuses

## Branch interaction rules

### Politics and economy

- Lemass industrialisation can operate under Fianna Fáil, coalition government, or a later social republic, but ownership and labour effects change.
- Fine Gael fiscal policy lowers external risk and slows rapid public expansion.
- Labour converts industrial growth into union, welfare, and ownership decisions.
- corporatist routes replace bargaining with compulsory sector bodies.
- revolutionary routes rely on requisition, local committees, and foreign or captured equipment.

### Politics and defence

- constitutional routes strengthen professional command
- reconciliation routes integrate local and republican defence networks
- Labour creates citizen defence and workplace mobilisation
- Allied routes create interoperability and access
- IRA and corporate routes create rival military institutions

### Politics and diplomacy

- neutrality is easier under broad constitutional consent
- Allied entry requires parliamentary or revolutionary justification
- foreign aid changes internal legitimacy
- Catholic diplomacy can support several routes but conflicts with radical left and some fascist aims

### Politics and Northern Ireland

- constitutional routes favour negotiation, federalism, or international guarantees
- Labour can build cross-border social and union coalitions
- IRA favours coercive or insurrectionary paths
- O'Duffy and Ailtirí pursue authoritarian unity with severe resistance
- hidden provincial routes can transform unity into a confederal or impossible settlement

### Society and hidden content

- cultural policy creates hidden discovery opportunities
- coercive language policy can empower Ailtirí
- inclusive language policy can support federal or pan-Celtic cultural outcomes
- folklore and regional development can support mythic discovery without requiring authoritarianism

## Localisation direction principles

Final text must distinguish route voices without writing caricatures.

### Constitutional republican voice

- precise, sovereign, patient, legal, and historically conscious
- avoids generic revolutionary slogans after state consolidation

### Fine Gael constitutional voice

- institutional, legal, security-minded, and fiscally cautious
- avoids treating every British contact as loyalty to Britain

### Labour voice

- material, organisational, social, and rooted in workers, housing, wages, and public service
- avoids generic imported ideological vocabulary when Irish labour language fits better

### Republican revolutionary voice

- claims continuity, sacrifice, partition, and unfinished sovereignty
- distinguishes Army Council militarism from social republicanism

### Catholic vocational voice

- moral, communal, occupational, and paternal
- reveals coercion through policy consequences rather than empty sinister language

### O'Duffy and Ailtirí voice

- period authoritarian, anti-communist, corporatist, and coercive
- Ailtirí additionally uses radical Gaelic purification and total mobilisation
- the writing must not conceal fascist ideology behind picturesque cultural language

### Hidden absurd voice

- begins with grounded folklore, regional practice, uncertainty, and material consequences
- does not announce that a supernatural route exists
- final impossible content can become ceremonial, mythic, unsettling, or exuberantly absurd according to the specific branch
