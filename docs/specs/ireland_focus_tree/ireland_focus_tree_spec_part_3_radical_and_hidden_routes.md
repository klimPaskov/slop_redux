## Classification rule

Every historical claim and divergence uses one of three labels.

| Label | Meaning | Part 3 use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `H` | documented history | real people, movements, programmes, laws, organisations, events, strengths, and weaknesses |
| `P` | plausible speculation | a route that grows from those actors and pressures but did not occur in the designed form |
| `A` | deliberate absurdity | a culturally grounded impossible, supernatural, or intentionally extreme outcome |

The transition from `H` to `P` to `A` must be visible in hidden routes. A working route name never turns fiction into a historical claim.

## Part 3 design result

Part 3 completes six radical and hidden political families.

1. The IRA Army Council route converts an armed clandestine organisation into a contested governing authority.
2. The Republican Congress route rebuilds a broken socialist-republican coalition through unions, local committees, agrarian actors, and an explicit extra-parliamentary transition.
3. The O'Duffy route reconstructs a marginal corporate movement after Spain and requires crisis, veterans, patrons, organisation, and army acceptance.
4. The clerical and corporate transformation consumes the failure of lawful vocational government and can become a producer state, guardianship state, emergency commission, or captured council state.
5. Ailtirí emerges through dated wartime cells, cultural infiltration, publishing, youth recruitment, patrons, and public launch after June 1942.
6. Serious cultural state-building supports three concealed restoration families: High Kingship, Five Provinces, and the Otherworld. Each remains useful when its hidden reveal never occurs.

Part 3 does not design the complete economy, armed forces, active neutrality, foreign alignment, Northern Ireland settlement, external expansion, or late-game international order. Part 4 owns those systems and receives exact route handoffs here.

## Parliamentary and constitutional inheritance

- The Dáil opens with `153` formal seats and `152` routine confidence votes.
- Fianna Fáil holds `77` formal seats and `76` routine government votes because the outgoing Ceann Comhairle occupies one seat.
- The Fine Gael predecessor inheritance is `59` seats, Labour holds `8`, and other deputies hold `9`.
- Party popularity never substitutes for seats.
- Party leader, parliamentary leader, Taoiseach, cabinet office-holder, head of state, security commander, and coalition sponsor can be different people.
- The 1937 plebiscite and Dáil election remain separate results.
- A radical takeover preserves the record of the President, Taoiseach, cabinet, Dáil, Seanad, parties, coalitions, laws, and elections it displaces.
- A recovery route must restore or replace those institutions explicitly. It cannot pretend they never existed.

## Radical route access doctrine

Radical routes are consequences of political work and crisis. They do not sit beside constitutional routes as equal buttons in January 1936.

A valid transition has five layers.

| Layer | Required proof | Invalid shortcut |
| --- | --- | --- |
| organisation | real networks, cadres, branches, unions, councils, cells, commands, or patrons | ideology popularity alone |
| crisis | a named constitutional, security, social, wartime, or council failure | one focus declaring emergency |
| public base | Social Consent, local committees, voters, members, clients, or coercive control appropriate to the route | assumed national support |
| state interface | Dáil collapse, cabinet failure, security division, council capture, or lawful constituent process | free government office |
| transition | event and mission sequence that transforms the National Settlement and leadership records | flag or cosmetic name only |

The player can deliberately create some enabling conditions, but the cost appears through failed institutions, repression, lost consent, equipment, organisation, foreign dependency, and route-specific enemies. A crisis route with no cost is a reward ladder and fails this specification.

## Shared transition protocol

Every radical takeover or hidden state transformation follows this order.

1. Freeze the current government ledger and record every office-holder, party, coalition, law, and active crisis.
2. Resolve whether the transition is a seizure, negotiated convention, council transfer, public launch, referendum, or hidden reveal.
3. Preserve the National Settlement value, clamp it to `-60` through `+60`, and apply the route-specific pole labels.
4. Remove contributors that belonged only to the defeated route. Preserve common values and historical records.
5. Set the provisional government, movement leadership, public authority, and security command as separate offices where relevant.
6. Begin a six-month review with route-specific success, partial success, and failure bands.
7. Open only the decisions, missions, events, advisors, and focus groups supported by the new government state.
8. Run cleanup for abandoned elections, coalitions, councils, cells, missions, and temporary offices.
9. At conclusion, merge or replace route spirits so the country retains no more than three focus-created persistent route spirits.

# IRA Army Council route

Working structural label: `The Army Council Claims the Republic` (working label)

Historical classification: `H` organisational substrate with `P` takeover and state-building outcomes

## IRA Army Council route: Player promise

The player converts a clandestine armed organisation into a governing authority while surviving factional rivalry, state penetration, military weakness, public mistrust, and the danger of foreign sponsorship. The central question is whether armed legitimacy can submit to a civil republican settlement before the organisation destroys the state it claims to complete.

## IRA Army Council route: Access gate

- The National Settlement is in `Revolutionary Command`, or it has remained in `Armed Dual Authority` through two failed recovery cycles.
- Republican Network Strength is at least `75` for a direct Army Council bid, or at least `65` for a negotiated dual-authority convention.
- Security Loyalty is `40` or lower, or a named security crisis has divided the Garda, army, or cabinet.
- At least one lawful republican settlement has failed, such as a collapsed amnesty, rejected constitutional convention, indiscriminate repression crisis, or unresolved partition mobilisation.
- Social Consent is below `50` for a direct seizure. A negotiated convention may begin at higher Consent if the Dáil has lost a valid government and local authorities recognise republican committees.
- No foreign occupier controls Dublin. Foreign sponsorship cannot substitute for domestic organisation.

## IRA Army Council route: Route disqualifiers

- A stable constitutional government with Dáil Mandate at least `60`, Security Loyalty at least `60`, and Republican Network Strength below `55`.
- A completed constitutional reconciliation settlement that has disarmed the relevant commands and passed its six-month review.
- The Republican Congress route has already subordinated armed groups to its constituent settlement.
- A foreign puppet or occupation government controls the Irish administration.

## IRA Army Council route: National Settlement transformation

## IRA Army Council route: Recovery paths

- A fragmented command can call a protected convention if at least two regional commands, one civil network, and a neutral guarantor survive.
- A military dictatorship can move toward a civil republic only by releasing political detainees, accepting command accountability, and completing a constituent mission.
- A compromised foreign channel can be severed through an intelligence purge that costs equipment, agents, and operational tempo.
- A failed seizure can return to constitutional politics through disarmament, amnesty, and an election. The route records the defeat and does not erase repression or casualties.

The player must rebuild a movement that had already fragmented, then keep trade unions, socialist republicans, Labour dissidents, small farmers, local committees, and armed anti-fascists inside one common programme. - The IRA Army Council has subordinated labour organisations through armed command.

- A foreign communist or fascist sponsor directly controls the organising convention.

- Gaeltacht districts provide living communities with different dialects, economic needs, transport problems, education pressures, and reasons to distrust symbolic policy that lacks services.
- Universities, libraries, museums, local historical societies, theatre, broadcasting, music, sport, and publishing provide routes for urban and rural cultural participation.
- Royal sites, manuscript collections, place-name traditions, and early Irish legal material provide historical objects of study. They do not provide proof that a medieval institution can govern a modern state.

## Design rule for cultural legitimacy

Culture has three possible political relationships.

| Relationship | Meaning | Gameplay result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| plural stewardship | institutions serve communities and preserve disagreement | higher Social Consent, Minority Confidence, capacity, and long-term resilience |
| state direction | government coordinates schools, broadcasting, archives, and regional investment | faster delivery with administrative and consent costs |
| political capture | one movement uses cultural bodies for recruitment, exclusion, or rule | route power rises, plural legitimacy falls, resistance and institutional flight grow |

No political route owns Irish culture by default. Fianna Fáil, Fine Gael, Labour, republican, corporate, Ailtirí, royal, provincial, and hidden governments must earn cooperation from the institutions they use.

The public interface uses one working label, `Cultural Capacity`. It presents four named components. Each component ranges from `0` to `100` and has visible causes, thresholds, and regional notes. The aggregate is a presentation summary, not a substitute for the components.

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

| Band | State | Consequence |
| --- | --- | --- |

## Route interactions

### Constitutional governments

They can use the lane to increase state capacity and legitimacy. The strongest democratic result combines bilingual service, plural cultural bodies, local participation, and public archives. Executive overreach can still convert the lane into symbolic coercion.

### Labour and Congress

They gain workplace teaching, union education, community theatres, cooperative publishing, rural libraries, and social-history collection. Congress can use local committees for delivery, but a party archive that excludes rival testimony reduces Coalition Cohesion.

### IRA government

It can use republican commemorations, local networks, and all-island contacts. Armed control of collectors or sites increases security reach and damages archival trust. The civil-republican side can protect plural institutions and gain legitimacy.

### O'Duffy and corporate government

They can sponsor pageantry, vocational arts, rural societies, and Catholic social institutions. Political capture, leader cults, and selective history reduce scholarly credibility and Minority Confidence.

### Ailtirí

Ailtirí can infiltrate the same institutions. Its route gains Cell Penetration from captured branches and publications. Serious investment still creates real capacity. Coercion, antisemitism, one-party control, and the English-language deadline convert that capacity into a contested state system with severe social costs.

### Northern Ireland and Part 4

Cultural contacts can prepare later negotiations, local trust, place-name work, education agreements, and provincial representation. They cannot create cores, annexation, unity, or automatic Northern consent.

# Concealed restoration doctrine

## Purpose

The concealed routes reward sustained cultural, regional, and institutional work under unusual political conditions. They do not appear as novelty branches in January 1936. Each route has a serious public lane, a speculative constitutional or political stage, and an impossible stage where relevant.

The three families are:

1. High Kingship
2. Five Provinces
3. The Otherworld

Early evidence can overlap. Route commitment eventually creates real exclusions and convergence rules.

## Reveal stack

A concealed family becomes available only after five layers are satisfied.

| Layer | Requirement | Player visibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| public capacity | named Language Service, Archival Record, Regional Participation, and Site Stewardship thresholds | fully visible |
| historical work | route-specific surveys, manuscripts, local bodies, and site protections | fully visible |
| political opening | constitutional crisis, constituent process, regional breakdown, occupation shock, succession crisis, or unusual government state | visible when it exists |
| evidence pattern | route-specific sequence of reports, artefacts, testimonies, ceremonies, or anomalies | partial and uncertain until accumulated |
| commitment event | a council, assembly, circuit, compact, or contact that asks the government to cross the route boundary | explicit choice with classification shift |

A high capacity score without evidence gives strong serious cultural rewards. A strange event without capacity produces a false lead, local story, or contained incident.

## Evidence ledgers

Evidence is stored by family. The interface reveals a family ledger only after the first credible item.

| Family | Evidence types | Credibility sources | Common false leads |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| High Kingship | royal-site continuity, inauguration material, legal opinions, provincial assent, claimant tests | protected sites, manuscript commission, recognised councils, public witnesses | forged pedigree, political pageant, tourist spectacle, leader cult |
| Five Provinces | provincial mandates, Meath or middle-country identity, regional charters, shared-site assembly, local guard agreements | county councils, festivals, regional organisations, all-island delegates | Dublin-created paper provinces, patronage blocs, one-county separatism |
| Otherworld | repeated place-lore correspondence, impossible archival consistency, site anomalies, fulfilled obligations, independent witnesses | high archival quality, protected sites, multiple regions, uncensored records | hoax, wartime deception, smuggling signal, propaganda, mass suggestion |

The player can investigate, preserve, publish, seal, exploit, or reject evidence. Exploitation can accelerate a route and corrupt its legitimacy.

## False lead rules

A false lead remains useful content.

- A forged pedigree can expose patron networks and improve archive standards.
- A supposed royal relic can become a museum asset after authentication fails.
- A provincial separatist scare can reveal real local grievances.
- A reported mound light can uncover a smuggling route, military signal, or weather phenomenon.
- A claimed Fianna sighting can lead to a local defence club, folklore volume, or hoax prosecution.
- A disputed legal manuscript can still advance scholarship after the political claim fails.

False leads never consume the player's serious investment without a result. They do not provide the hidden route reward.

## Shared disqualifiers

Concealed routes close or change when:

- the relevant sites are destroyed or held by a hostile occupier without a recovery path
- cultural institutions are fully captured by a party that suppresses independent evidence
- Archival Record is below `30` and no credible chain can be verified
- Regional Participation is below `30` for a route claiming national or provincial assent
- a foreign sponsor appoints the claimant, council, or supernatural interpreter
- the government fabricates evidence and suppresses the correction
- another concealed family has reached an incompatible sovereignty conclusion

A coercive government can still discover strange events. It receives exploitation, repression, or containment branches rather than automatic legitimate restoration.

## Hidden AI prohibition

Historical AI has zero weight for every concealed commitment event.

Rare alternate AI requires all of the following:

- nonhistorical mode or an explicit hidden-content rule
- complete public cultural prerequisites
- valid political opening
- credible evidence ledger
- no route disqualifier
- an AI profile assigned to the specific family

Once committed, the AI receives a complete plan. It must understand obligations, map objectives, route values, failure recovery, and Part 4 handoffs. A rare route cannot rely on human-only clicks.

## Hidden route cleanup

Every investigation records:

- active site targets
- assigned guards or researchers
- temporary local authorities
- evidence ownership
- public knowledge state
- route claimants or delegates
- deadlines and obligations

On rejection, defeat, annexation, route change, site loss, or conclusion, the system clears obsolete targets and converts useful public institutions back into serious cultural content. The archive, teachers, roads, museums, and regional councils do not vanish because a hidden claim failed.

# High Kingship restoration

Working structural label: `The Crown of Tara` (working label)

Classification: `H` research basis, `P` constitutional restoration, `A` sacral and impossible outcomes

## High Kingship restoration: Player promise

The player turns scholarship, royal-site protection, regional assent, and a crisis of republican authority into a contested modern kingship. The serious route asks whether a symbolic high king can provide unity without destroying the constitutional state. The absurd route asks what happens when inauguration traditions, the Lia Fáil, and the obligations of sacral kingship begin to produce results that ordinary politics cannot explain.

## High Kingship restoration: Historical to speculative boundary

### High Kingship restoration: Historical to speculative boundary: Documented history

- Tara has a documented long-term association with high kingship and later national symbolism.
- The Lia Fáil is associated in tradition with inauguration and is presented at Tara as a legendary object.
- Early Irish law, royal genealogies, inauguration traditions, learned families, and royal sites survive through manuscripts, archaeology, scholarship, and local memory.
- A modern Ireland can study, protect, commemorate, and reinterpret these materials.

### High Kingship restoration: Historical to speculative boundary: Plausible speculation

- A constitutional crisis could produce proposals for a ceremonial or elective high kingship.
- A constituent assembly could create a non-hereditary crown, a royal council, or a time-limited guardian office.
- Provincial and local bodies could require a national figure to take an oath to the Constitution, minority safeguards, and regional autonomy.
- A modern royal office could use historical symbolism while remaining accountable to law.

### High Kingship restoration: Historical to speculative boundary: Deliberate absurdity

- The Lia Fáil identifies or rejects a claimant through an impossible event.
- Royal sites impose binding obligations that produce supernatural consequences when broken.
- A high king gains authority from the land, weather, or legendary treasures.
- historical figures, divine beings, or impossible claimants return as literal political actors

The specification never presents the absurd layer as evidence about real Irish history or belief.

## High Kingship restoration: Access and reveal conditions

### High Kingship restoration: Access and reveal conditions: Public preparation

The route requires all of the following:

- Site Stewardship at least `65`
- Archival Record at least `60`
- Regional Participation at least `55`
- the royal-site survey complete at Tara and at least three additional recognised sites
- the early-law and manuscript commission complete
- a public rule protecting religious and political pluralism, or an explicit coercive alternative with corresponding legitimacy penalties

### High Kingship restoration: Access and reveal conditions: Political opening

At least one opening is required.

| Opening | Requirement | Route effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| constitutional guardianship | presidency or executive succession crisis with a functioning constituent process | favours elective and ceremonial kingship |
| broken republic | two failed governments, low Dáil Mandate, and no stable radical victor | favours crown as emergency settlement |
| all-island symbolic convention | Northern and southern cultural delegates accept a shared non-territorial ceremony | favours federal and plural crown |
| victorious restoration government | a radical or corporate government seeks a wider legitimacy settlement | opens appropriation, controlled crown, or genuine break branches |
| national survival crisis | Dublin threatened, state institutions dispersed, and royal sites protected by local authorities | favours guardian and war-leadership claims |

No opening grants office automatically. A claim phase follows.

### Evidence gate

The route needs four credible royal items from at least three categories.

- recognised inauguration or legal continuity evidence
- provincial or regional assent
- public testimony from a protected royal site
- a claimant or council that passes background and independence review
- a successful national circuit
- a route-specific event involving the Lia Fáil

The first five can reveal the plausible route. The impossible Lia Fáil branch requires a separate explicit choice after the plausible claim is already active.

## Claimants and leadership forms

The route avoids unsupported claims about living dynasties. Implementation may research real historical or genealogical claims, but no person becomes implementation-ready without source review and political plausibility.

The default claimant forms are institutional.

| Form | Classification | Selection | Leadership structure |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| elected Ard-Rí | `P` | constituent convention and provincial ballot | high king, Taoiseach, and Dáil remain separate offices |
| guardian crown | `P` | emergency convention for a fixed term | crown protects succession and constitutional continuity |
| royal council | `P` | recognised sites and provincial delegates choose a collective office | collective portrait, rotating chair, no hereditary succession |
| constitutional house | `P` | a researched claimant is invited and accepts strict law | crown, ministry, and legislature remain distinct |
| stone-chosen ruler | `A` | impossible inauguration result | ruler remains subject to geasa and route obligations |
| returned sovereign | `A` | deep Otherworld convergence | supernatural or legendary figure with generated identity and explicit absurd classification |

A current President can serve as regent, guardian, or convention chair. That role does not make the real person a monarch without an explicit alternate-history decision.

## High Kingship restoration: National Settlement transformation

The left pole becomes **Sacral Sovereignty**. The right pole becomes **Elective Constitutional Kingship**.

| Range | Route stage | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `-100` to `-76` | Unbounded Sacred Rule | the ruler claims authority above law and institution |
| `-75` to `-46` | Royal Command | crown, household, and site guardians dominate the state |

The stable target depends on route intent. A constitutional outcome targets `+20` to `+50`. A sacral route targets `-20` to `-45` and must manage obligations. Either extreme can become a failure.

## High Kingship restoration: Core route values

### Royal Legitimacy

Measures acceptance of the crown by regions, institutions, public bodies, minority communities, security forces, and foreign observers.

Sources:

- valid constituent process
- provincial assent
- fulfilled public obligations
- restrained household spending
- protection of sites and archives
- nonpartisan succession
- successful wartime guardianship

Losses:

- fabricated genealogy
- foreign appointment
- sectarian or party crown
- broken oath
- household corruption
- failed circuit
- coercive coronation

### Obligation Burden

Measures the accumulated duties attached to kingship. In the plausible route these are legal and political. In the absurd route they become geasa with direct consequences.

Examples:

- hold a public assembly on schedule
- protect a named province or vulnerable community
- maintain access to a royal site
- refuse a foreign patron's unlawful demand
- hear petitions from every province
- avoid a named form of coercion
- complete a circuit before a deadline
- prevent hunger or displacement in a named region

A strong crown gains power from accepted obligations. Ignoring them increases crisis pressure, regional refusal, and impossible consequences in the absurd route.

### Household Reach

Tracks the crown's appointments, ceremonial network, guards, estates, publications, patronage, and influence. High reach can support national coordination. It can also create a parallel state and patronage machine.

## Crown convention

The route begins with a multi-stage convention.

### Credentials

Delegates come from:

- Dáil parties and independents if the Dáil still functions
- county and provincial bodies
- cultural and legal institutions
- recognised minority and church representatives
- labour and rural organisations
- security and defence observers without a controlling vote
- northern cultural delegates where lawful and willing
- royal-site guardian bodies

A coercive regime can exclude groups. Each exclusion reduces legitimacy and changes later branches.

### Crown form

The player chooses among elective, fixed-term guardian, collective royal council, researched dynastic, and controlled ceremonial forms. Stone-chosen and returned-sovereign forms remain hidden.

### Powers

The convention allocates:

- appointment and dismissal rules
- emergency powers
- legislative assent or referral
- military command
- judicial and constitutional guardianship
- regional mediation
- succession
- finance and household limits
- religious neutrality
- minority guarantees

### Ratification

Plausible routes require a Dáil vote, constituent assembly, referendum, or equivalent public process. An armed regime can impose the settlement, then begins with low legitimacy and active resistance.

- Stories of the Tuatha Dé Danann, the sí, Fionn and the Fianna, sovereignty figures, enchanted places, omens, and encounters existed in literary and oral traditions with major regional variation.
- Governments can investigate rumours, protect sites, manage crowds, and distinguish folklore from espionage, smuggling, weather, crime, and military signals.

### The Otherworld route: Exact classification boundary: Plausible speculation

- A wartime government could create a small interdisciplinary office for unexplained reports around protected sites.
- Folklorists, Garda officers, military intelligence, clergy, doctors, local officials, and scientists could disagree over evidence and public policy.
- The state could seal records, protect witnesses, stop exploitation, or use unexplained events as propaganda.
- Repeated incidents could create public belief, panic, pilgrimage, or local resistance even when no supernatural explanation is established.

### The Otherworld route: Exact classification boundary: Deliberate absurdity

The route crosses into `A` only after an event in which independently gathered records contain the same detailed information before the witnesses, collectors, and offices could have communicated. The working reveal event is `The Archive Records What Has Not Yet Happened`.

From that event onward, impossible actors and effects may become real within the campaign. They remain fictional game content.

## Access conditions

### Public cultural requirements

- Archival Record at least `75`
- Site Stewardship at least `70`
- Regional Participation at least `60`
- Language Service at least `45`, because several records and local testimonies require competent interpretation
- the ethics and local-copy policies completed, or an authoritarian seizure policy that creates corresponding distrust and distorted evidence
- at least four county or regional collection drives completed without unresolved consent failure
- at least four protected sites in different regions

### Political and campaign requirements

At least two of the following unusual conditions:

- Ireland has maintained neutrality through at least two years of general European war while suffering repeated air, naval, intelligence, or supply incidents
- the national government has temporarily dispersed outside Dublin
- a royal or provincial convention has assembled at a recognised site
- three separate regions have completed emergency survival missions without central assistance
- a foreign intelligence operation has used folklore, pilgrimage, or local custom as cover and has been exposed
- a serious censorship conflict has left part of the archive sealed
- a major storm, bombing, mobilisation, or evacuation has produced simultaneous reports from isolated districts
- a protected site has been threatened by construction, looting, occupation, or military use and saved before the deadline

The conditions create a campaign context. They do not prove the impossible event.

### Evidence requirements

The route needs six credible evidence items from at least four categories and four regions.

| Category | Examples | Verification rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| archive correspondence | matching stories, place names, dates, or obligations in independent collections | collector and informant chains must be separate |
| site anomaly | physical change, repeated sound, light, absence, or impossible access pattern | site must be protected and inspected by more than one institution |
| fulfilled local warning | a recorded warning predicts a specific local event | the record must predate the event and survive custody review |
| impossible witness agreement | unrelated witnesses provide the same unusual detail | no shared publication, police interview, or political organiser |
| object or manuscript inconsistency | material has a date, text, or condition that cannot be reconciled | scholarly and forensic review required |
| obligation response | a public action at one site produces a linked result elsewhere | at least two regions and a controlled comparison |

A single dramatic report is never enough.

## Pre-reveal investigation gameplay

Before the absurd threshold, every incident receives one of five classifications.

1. ordinary local explanation
2. criminal or smuggling activity
3. foreign intelligence or military deception
4. unresolved cultural report
5. credible anomaly

A report can move between classifications after new evidence. Publicly declaring the supernatural before the threshold damages archival trust and can close the legitimate route.

### Pre-reveal decisions

| Decision | Cost | Result | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Reveal sequence

The full reveal is a chain, not one popup.

### `The Unrelated Pages`

Collectors find matching detailed accounts in sealed or geographically separated records. The state can dismiss, audit custody, or convene a mixed inquiry.

### `The Witnesses Who Never Met`

Independent witnesses repeat a phrase, obligation, or description that has not been published. The player chooses witness protection, public hearing, security interrogation, or silence.

### `The Event in the Record`

A specific event occurs after it was documented in an authenticated earlier record. Ordinary explanations remain possible but strained.

### `The Archive Records What Has Not Yet Happened`

A second controlled prediction succeeds under conditions that defeat communication, forgery, and foreign deception. This is the explicit `H/P` to `A` boundary.

The player then chooses:

- recognise contact and seek a compact
- seal the evidence and contain the sites
- exploit the contact for state power
- reject the interpretation and continue human investigation

Rejection keeps the world absurd because the impossible event occurred. It creates a human-restoration branch rather than returning the classification to history.

## The Otherworld route: Core route values

### Veil Stability

Measures whether contact remains localised, comprehensible, and governed by known obligations.

Veil Stability rises through:

- protected sites
- honoured access limits
- accurate archives
- fulfilled obligations
- local guardian cooperation
- controlled public information
- prevention of foreign exploitation

It falls through:

- site damage
- broken geasa
- forced extraction
- mass panic
- foreign intrusion
- contradictory bargains
- political fabrication
- rapid expansion of contact without administration

At low Veil Stability, incidents spread and route actions become less predictable. At zero, a breach crisis begins.

### Compact Trust

Tracks reciprocal confidence between human institutions, local communities, and the specific Otherworld actors recognised by the route.

High trust allows negotiated aid, passage, warning, and guardianship. Low trust produces withholding, misdirection, retaliation, or domination.

### Obligation Ledger

Every bargain creates a visible obligation with owner, scope, deadline, success, partial success, failure, and cleanup. Obligations can be legal, regional, environmental, ceremonial, military, or personal to the governing office.

No route power is free. The strongest impossible rewards carry the most difficult continuing obligations.

## Public law and rights

The route must answer ordinary legal questions even after the impossible reveal.

- Who has legal personhood and standing?
- Which human rights remain unconditional?
- Can an obligation override criminal, labour, property, religious, or minority law?
- Who may enter or close a site?
- Who owns an object returned through contact?
- Can a local council bargain for the whole state?
- Does military necessity override the compact?
- How are witnesses protected from detention, exploitation, and publicity?
- What happens when an Otherworld actor harms a citizen or a citizen harms a protected site?
- Can the government refuse a geis that violates public law?

The balanced compact preserves human rights and allows negotiated refusal. Dominion and exploitation routes answer differently and pay explicit costs.

## Church, belief, and minority rules

No church receives automatic authority over the phenomenon. Catholic, Protestant, Jewish, secular, folkloric, scientific, and local interpretations remain distinct.

- Church institutions can provide pastoral care and local access.

- Minority Confidence falls if the state turns one interpretation into compulsory belief.
- Vatican Leverage changes through diplomatic and doctrinal reaction, not ownership of the sites.
- Ailtirí and clerical states can attempt ideological capture, which reduces Compact Trust and increases exclusion.
- Congress can frame contact through common stewardship and labour, though it still must respect local belief and evidence.
