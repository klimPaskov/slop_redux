# Ireland BOP range matrix

| Band tier | Suggested threshold | Shared design effect | Implementation note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| first side extreme | 75 to 100 toward first side | first side can overreach, challenger routes are blocked or angry | use strong benefits and real tradeoffs |
| first side high | 45 to 74 toward first side | first side sets policy, decisions from that side cheaper or safer | route tools should be reliable but not free |
| first side moderate | 15 to 44 toward first side | first side has momentum | best band for stable legal route play |
| contested center | 14 either direction | route flexibility, but major actions need preparation | avoid empty no effect center |
| second side moderate | 15 to 44 toward second side | challenger has momentum | unlocks route tools with risk |
| second side high | 45 to 74 toward second side | challenger shapes policy | crisis missions and stronger route actions show |
| second side extreme | 75 to 100 toward second side | takeover, collapse, or forced crisis risk | use visible crisis, not tiny penalties |

## Mode specific extreme outcomes

| Mode | First extreme | Second extreme |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opening state | legal monopoly slows route flexibility and can radicalize old movements | public order collapse or radical route crisis |
| Historical emergency | cabinet refuses urgent powers and weakens wartime response | Emergency Directorate reveal or legitimacy crisis |
| Commonwealth cooperation | Britain cannot trust cooperation enough for bargains | dependency, base access scandal, domestic backlash |
| Labour republic | Labour base loses faith in reform | congress crisis, client pressure, production disruption |
| Blueshirt corporate | route softens into legal opposition or chamber state | guard capture, coup risk, foreign isolation |
| Republican underground | hard route disarmed or reconciled | compromised network, British pressure, Army Council takeover |
| Civic cultural | hidden route becomes slow and institutional | corrupted restoration failure |
| Emergency Directorate | emergency rule dismantled before threat is safe | permanent security state |
